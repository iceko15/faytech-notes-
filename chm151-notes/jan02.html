Atomic Structure & Stoichiometry

BIG IDEA

The atom is organized into discrete layers of energy and mass. Chemical behavior—specifically bonding and reactivity—is dictated by the configuration of the Valence Shell and the interaction of Cations and Anions.

[Image of atomic structure with nucleus and electron orbitals]

KEY TERMS

Term Definition
Isotopes Atoms of the same element with different numbers of neutrons in the nucleus.
Valence Shell The outermost electron layer responsible for chemical bonding.
Avogadro’s Number The constant \( 6.022 \times 10^{23} \), representing particles in one mole.
Electronegativity The measure of an atom's ability to attract shared electrons.

STEPS/PROCESS: MOLE TRANSFORMATIONS

  1. Identify Given Mass: Start with the horizontal mass shift (grams).
  2. Convert to Moles: Use Moles = \( \frac{\text{mass}}{\text{molar mass}} \).
  3. Vertical Translation: Use the molar ratio from a balanced synthesis equation to shift between reactants and products.
  4. Final Conversion: Translate moles into volume or number of particles using Avogadro's Number.

RULES: THE PERIODIC SYSTEM

  • The Nucleus: Contains protons and neutrons; defines the identity and mass of the atom.
  • Electron Cloud: Organized into subshells (s, p, d, f) and orbitals.
  • Ionic Layer: Bonding occurs when atoms transfer electrons to become Cations (positive) or Anions (negative).
  • Molar Mass: Calculated by summing the atomic weights of all atoms in a molecule.

EXAMPLE/DEMONSTRATION

1. Molar Mass Calculation

For \( H_2O \): \[ (2 \times 1.008) + 16.00 = 18.016 \text{ g/mol} \]

2. Mass to Mole Conversion

How many moles are in 36g of water? \[ 36\text{g} \times \frac{1 \text{ mole}}{18.016\text{g}} \approx 2 \text{ moles} \]

3. Avogadro's Application

Total molecules in 2 moles: \[ 2 \times (6.022 \times 10^{23}) = 1.2044 \times 10^{24} \text{ molecules} \]

PRACTICE PROBLEMS

  1. Which subatomic particles are found in the nucleus? (Protons and Neutrons)
  2. What is the charge of an Anion? (Negative)
  3. Convert 10.0g of an element with a molar mass of 20.0g/mol to moles. (0.5 moles)

SUMMARY

Understanding the "Atomic Layer" requires mastering both the physical structure (Nucleus and Valence Shell) and the mathematical "Mole-Transformation" cycle. High-periodic activity and bonding are essentially functions of electron movement and electronegativity.
