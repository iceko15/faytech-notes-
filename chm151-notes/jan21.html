BIG IDEA

Scientific measurements are never exact. Significant figures allow us to communicate the precision of our tools. In Chemistry, the limiting term (the least precise measurement) always dictates the precision of the final calculated answer.

1. SIGNIFICANT FIGURE COUNTS
  • a) 105: 3 Sig Figs
  • b) 0.82: 2 Sig Figs
  • c) 0.0069: 2 Sig Figs
  • d) 1500.: 4 Sig Figs
  • e) \(6.022 \times 10^{23}\): 4 Sig Figs
  • f) 2.0: 2 Sig Figs
2. ROUNDING (TO 3 SIG FIGS)
  • a) 0.08206 → 0.0821
  • b) 0.94658 → 0.947
  • c) 6.125 → 6.13
  • d) 7853 → 7850
  • e) 11838.88 → 11800
  • f) 19.998 → 20.0
3. EVALUATED EXPRESSIONS
a) \(75.2 - 68.3 = \mathbf{6.9}\)
b) \(0.9043 \times 0.0821 = \mathbf{0.0742}\)
c) \(185 \times \frac{26.0}{0.0604} \times \frac{22.4}{10.1} = \mathbf{1.77 \times 10^{5}}\)
d) \((2.54 + 9.4) \times \frac{3.142}{4.060} = \mathbf{9.21}\)
e) \((1.2095 - 1.0000) \times \frac{2.5400}{25.000} = \mathbf{0.02129}\)
f) \(\frac{[(7.82 \times 10^{-2}) + (8 \times 10^{-4})]}{(22.4 + 1.94)} = \mathbf{0.00325}\)
DIMENSIONAL ANALYSIS SET-UPS
4. Centimeters in 0.94 in
\(0.94 \text{ in} \times \left( \frac{2.54 \text{ cm}}{1 \text{ in}} \right) = \mathbf{2.4 \text{ cm}}\)
5. Convert 75 miles/hour to km/s
\(\frac{75 \text{ mi}}{1 \text{ hr}} \times \frac{1.609 \text{ km}}{1 \text{ mi}} \times \frac{1 \text{ hr}}{3600 \text{ s}} = \mathbf{0.034 \text{ km/s}}\)
9. Convert \(4.56 \times 10^{2} \text{ in}^{2}\) to \(\text{mm}^{2}\)
\(4.56 \times 10^{2} \text{ in}^{2} \times \left( \frac{2.54 \text{ cm}}{1 \text{ in}} \right)^{2} \times \left( \frac{10 \text{ mm}}{1 \text{ cm}} \right)^{2} = \mathbf{2.94 \times 10^{5} \text{ mm}^{2}}\)
DENSITY APPLICATIONS
11. Milliliters of lead in 2.00 lb sample
\(2.00 \text{ lb} \times \frac{0.4536 \text{ kg}}{1 \text{ lb}} \times \frac{1000 \text{ g}}{1 \text{ kg}} \times \frac{1 \text{ cm}^{3}}{11.34 \text{ g}} = \mathbf{80.0 \text{ mL}}\)
12. Mass of 1.00 L of Mercury
\(1.00 \text{ L} \times \frac{1000 \text{ mL}}{1 \text{ L}} \times \frac{13.6 \text{ g}}{1 \text{ mL}} = \mathbf{1.36 \times 10^{4} \text{ g}}\)
