Limiting Reactant Lab: Data & Calculations

REPORT FORM CALCULATIONS

1. Mass of $Na_2CO_3$ used:

Calculation:

  • Mass of beaker + sample: $112.2041\text{ g}$
  • Mass of empty beaker: $111.4658\text{ g}$
  • $\text{Difference} = 112.2041\text{ g} - 111.4658\text{ g} = \mathbf{0.7383\text{ g}}$

2. Mass of $CaCO_3$ collected:

Calculation:

  • Mass of filter paper + $CaCO_3$ + watch glass: $46.6461\text{ g}$
  • Mass of filter paper ($0.2365\text{ g}$) + watch glass ($45.8419\text{ g}$): $46.0784\text{ g}$
  • $\text{Difference} = 46.6461\text{ g} - 46.0784\text{ g} = \mathbf{0.5677\text{ g}}$

3. Balance the reaction:

$$1Na_2CO_3(aq) + 1CaCl_2(aq) \rightarrow 1CaCO_3(s) + 2NaCl(aq)$$

4. Calculate the theoretical yield of $CaCO_3$:

Step 1: Determine moles of $Na_2CO_3$

  • Molar Mass $Na_2CO_3 \approx 105.99\text{ g/mol}$
  • $n = 0.7383\text{ g} / 105.99\text{ g/mol} = \mathbf{0.006965\text{ mol}}$

Step 2: Determine moles of $CaCl_2$

  • Volume $= 30.0\text{ mL} = 0.0300\text{ L}$ (using the volume specified in the procedure)
  • $Molarity = 0.20\text{ M}$
  • $n = M \times V = 0.20\text{ mol/L} \times 0.0300\text{ L} = \mathbf{0.0060\text{ mol}}$

Step 3: Identify Limiting Reactant

  • The ratio is $1:1$. Since $0.0060\text{ mol}$ ($CaCl_2$) < $0.006965\text{ mol}$ ($Na_2CO_3$), $CaCl_2$ is the limiting reactant.

Step 4: Calculate Theoretical Mass

  • Theoretical moles of $CaCO_3 = 0.0060\text{ mol}$
  • Molar Mass $CaCO_3 \approx 100.09\text{ g/mol}$
  • $\text{Mass} = 0.0060\text{ mol} \times 100.09\text{ g/mol} = \mathbf{0.6005\text{ g}}$

5. Calculate the % yield of $CaCO_3$:

$$\text{Percent Yield} = \left( \frac{\text{Actual Yield}}{\text{Theoretical Yield}} \right) \times 100$$

$$\text{Percent Yield} = \left( \frac{0.5677\text{ g}}{0.6005\text{ g}} \right) \times 100 = \mathbf{94.54\%}$$

POST-LAB QUESTIONS

1. Describe two ways you could improve your percent yield if you completed this experiment a second time.

  • Ensure the precipitate is completely dry before weighing, as residual water increases "actual yield" incorrectly.
  • Use more DI water to rinse the beaker to ensure 100% of the precipitate is transferred to the filter funnel.

2. A student obtained a percent yield greater than 100%. What could have caused this error? What could be done to prevent this kind of error?

  • Cause: The precipitate was likely still wet. Water adds mass that isn't $CaCO_3$.
  • Prevention: Allow more time for vacuum filtration or use a drying oven/heat lamp to ensure all solvent (water/acetone) has evaporated.

3. A $115\text{ g}$ sample of silver nitrate is mixed with $125\text{ mL}$ of $6.0\text{ M}$ hydrochloric acid.

a. Write a balanced chemical reaction:

$$AgNO_3(aq) + HCl(aq) \rightarrow AgCl(s) + HNO_3(aq)$$

b. What is the theoretical yield of the precipitate?

  • Moles $AgNO_3 = 115\text{ g} / 169.87\text{ g/mol} = 0.677\text{ mol}$
  • Moles $HCl = 0.125\text{ L} \times 6.0\text{ mol/L} = 0.750\text{ mol}$
  • Limiting Reactant: $AgNO_3$ (since $0.677 < 0.750$)
  • Theoretical Mass $AgCl = 0.677\text{ mol} \times 143.32\text{ g/mol} = \mathbf{97.0\text{ g}}$

c. If the actual yield for silver chloride is $90.9\text{ g}$, what is the % yield?

$$\text{Percent Yield} = (90.9 / 97.0) \times 100 = \mathbf{93.7\%}$$

4. A $0.238\text{ g}$ sample of magnesium chloride reacts with $25.0\text{ mL}$ of $0.100\text{ M}$ potassium hydroxide.

a. Write a balanced chemical reaction:

$$MgCl_2(aq) + 2KOH(aq) \rightarrow Mg(OH)_2(s) + 2KCl(aq)$$

b. What is the theoretical yield of the precipitate?

  • Moles $MgCl_2 = 0.238\text{ g} / 95.21\text{ g/mol} = 0.00250\text{ mol}$
  • Moles $KOH = 0.0250\text{ L} \times 0.100\text{ mol/L} = 0.00250\text{ mol}$
  • Stoichiometry check: $0.00250\text{ mol } MgCl_2$ needs $0.00500\text{ mol } KOH$. We only have $0.00250$.
  • Limiting Reactant: $KOH$
  • Yield: $0.00250\text{ mol KOH} \times (1\text{ mol } Mg(OH)_2 / 2\text{ mol } KOH) = 0.00125\text{ mol } Mg(OH)_2$
  • Mass: $0.00125\text{ mol} \times 58.32\text{ g/mol} = \mathbf{0.0729\text{ g}}$

c. If actual yield is $0.0500\text{ g}$, what is the % yield?

$$\text{Percent Yield} = (0.0500 / 0.0729) \times 100 = \mathbf{68.6\%}$$

5. A $1.97\text{ g}$ sample of barium hydroxide octahydrate reacts with $25.0\text{ mL}$ of $0.100\text{ M}$ phosphoric acid. What is the % yield if actual yield is $0.700\text{ g}$?

$$3Ba(OH)_2\cdot8H_2O(s) + 2H_3PO_4(aq) \rightarrow Ba_3(PO_4)_2(s) + 30H_2O(l)$$

  • Moles $Ba(OH)_2\cdot8H_2O = 1.97\text{ g} / 315.46\text{ g/mol} = 0.006245\text{ mol}$
  • Moles $H_3PO_4 = 0.0250\text{ L} \times 0.100\text{ mol/L} = 0.00250\text{ mol}$
  • Check Limiting: $(0.006245 / 3) = 0.00208$ vs $(0.00250 / 2) = 0.00125$.
  • Limiting Reactant: $H_3PO_4$
  • Theoretical Yield: $(0.00250\text{ mol } H_3PO_4 / 2) \times 1 = 0.00125\text{ mol } Ba_3(PO_4)_2$
  • Mass: $0.00125\text{ mol} \times 601.93\text{ g/mol} = 0.7524\text{ g}$
  • % Yield: $(0.700 / 0.7524) \times 100 = \mathbf{93.0\%}$$

6. Silver nitrate ($0.425\text{ g}$) reacts with $25.0\text{ mL}$ of $0.100\text{ M}$ potassium phosphate. $87.3\%$ yield. How many grams isolated?

  • Reaction: $3AgNO_3 + K_3PO_4 \rightarrow Ag_3(PO_4) + 3KNO_3$
  • Moles $AgNO_3 = 0.425 / 169.87 = 0.00250\text{ mol}$
  • Moles $K_3PO_4 = 0.025 \times 0.100 = 0.00250\text{ mol}$
  • Limiting: $AgNO_3$. Theoretical moles $Ag_3PO_4 = 0.00250 / 3 = 0.000833\text{ mol}$
  • Theoretical Mass: $0.000833 \times 418.58 = 0.3487\text{ g}$
  • Actual Isolated: $0.3487 \times 0.873 = \mathbf{0.304\text{ g}}$

7. $0.469\text{ g}$ Zinc bicarbonate reacts with $25.0\text{ mL}$ of $0.100\text{ M}$ $HCl$. How much $CO_2$?

$$Zn(HCO_3)_2 + 2HCl \rightarrow ZnCl_2 + 2CO_2 + 2H_2O$$

  • Moles $Zn(HCO_3)_2 = 0.469 / 187.42 = 0.00250\text{ mol}$
  • Moles $HCl = 0.025 \times 0.100 = 0.00250\text{ mol}$
  • Limiting: $HCl$. Theoretical moles $CO_2 = 0.00250\text{ mol } HCl \times (2\text{ mol } CO_2 / 2\text{ mol } HCl) = 0.00250\text{ mol}$
  • Mass: $0.00250 \times 44.01 = \mathbf{0.110\text{ g}}$

8. $0.315\text{ g}$ Sodium sulfite reacts with $25.0\text{ mL}$ of $0.100\text{ M}$ phosphoric acid. Theoretical yield $SO_2$?

$$3Na_2SO_3 + 2H_3PO_4 \rightarrow 2Na_3PO_4 + 3SO_2 + 3H_2O$$

  • Moles $Na_2SO_3 = 0.315 / 126.04 = 0.00250\text{ mol}$
  • Moles $H_3PO_4 = 0.025 \times 0.100 = 0.00250\text{ mol}$
  • Limiting: $Na_2SO_3$. Theoretical moles $SO_2 = 0.00250\text{ mol}$ (since ratio is $3:3$)
  • Mass: $0.00250 \times 64.06 = \mathbf{0.160\text{ g}}$
