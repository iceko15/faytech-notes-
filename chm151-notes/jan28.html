Chemical Reactions Lab & Post-Lab Analysis

BIG IDEA

The identification of chemical reactions through macroscopic observations (color change, precipitate formation, gas evolution, and heat) and the systematic translation of these observations into balanced chemical equations and reaction classifications.

POST-LAB QUESTIONS: IDENTIFICATION

Based on the observations recorded during the lab procedures:

  • A. Colorless liquid (procedure B.1): $H_2O(l)$ (Water)
  • B. Flame-extinguishing gas (procedure B.2): $CO_2(g)$ (Carbon dioxide)
  • C. Gray solid (procedure C.1): $Ag(s)$ (Silver metal)
  • D. Colorless gas (procedure C.2): $H_2(g)$ (Hydrogen gas)
  • E. Orange precipitate (procedure D.2): $HgI_2(s)$ (Mercury (II) iodide)
  • F. Yellow precipitate (procedure D.4): $Ag_3PO_4(s)$ (Silver phosphate)
  • G. White precipitate (procedure D.6): $AlPO_4(s)$ (Aluminum phosphate)

BALANCING & CLASSIFICATION

A. Burning Copper (Formation of Oxide)
$$2Cu(s) + O_2(g) \rightarrow 2CuO(s)$$ Reaction type: Combination / Combustion
B. Mercury (II) Nitrate + Potassium Bromide
$$Hg_2(NO_3)_2(aq) + 2KBr(aq) \rightarrow Hg_2Br_2(s) + 2KNO_3(aq)$$ Reaction type: Double Replacement (Precipitation)
C. Aluminum + Sulfuric Acid
$$2Al(s) + 3H_2SO_4(aq) \rightarrow Al_2(SO_4)_3(aq) + 3H_2(g)$$ Reaction type: Single Replacement
D. Acetic Acid + Barium Hydroxide
$$2CH_3COOH(aq) + Ba(OH)_2(aq) \rightarrow Ba(CH_3COO)_2(aq) + 2H_2O(l)$$ Reaction type: Neutralization (Double Replacement)
E. Iron (III) Carbonate Decomposition
$$Fe_2(CO_3)_3(s) \rightarrow Fe_2O_3(s) + 3CO_2(g)$$ Reaction type: Decomposition
F. Sodium + Water
$$2Na(s) + 2H_2O(l) \rightarrow 2NaOH(aq) + H_2(g)$$ Reaction type: Single Replacement
G. Calcium Chloride Dihydrate Heating
$$CaCl_2 \cdot 2H_2O(s) \rightarrow CaCl_2(s) + 2H_2O(g)$$ Reaction type: Decomposition (Dehydration)

RULES/IDENTITIES

Precipitation Rules (Ref: Section D)

  • Silver Nitrate ($AgNO_3$): Often forms precipitates with halides ($AgI$ - Yellow) and phosphates ($Ag_3PO_4$ - Yellow).
  • Mercury (II) Nitrate ($Hg(NO_3)_2$): Reacts with Iodide to form a distinct orange $HgI_2$ precipitate.
  • Aluminum Nitrate ($Al(NO_3)_3$): Reacts with Sodium Phosphate to form white $AlPO_4$.

SUMMARY

This lab demonstrates the five primary types of chemical reactions: Combination (elements merging), Decomposition (compounds breaking down), Single Replacement (one element displacing another in a compound), Double Replacement (exchange of ions between two compounds), and Neutralization (acid + base reacting to form salt and water).
