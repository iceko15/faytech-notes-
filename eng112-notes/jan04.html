Discourse Communities & Final Formatting

BIG IDEA

Academic writing is defined by the Discourse Community it serves. Maintaining a specific "set point" for word count and a consistent formal tone ensures that your research remains within the acceptable professional range defined by the course rubric.

KEY TERMS

Term Definition
Discourse Community A group of people who share a set of discourses, understood as basic values and assumptions, and ways of communicating.
Set Point The target goal for a specific metric, such as the 1500-word count for the final paper.
Normal Range The acceptable margin of error (e.g., 1450–1600 words) where minor fluctuations do not negatively impact the grade.
Formality The level of professional tone required by the instructor to avoid the "red pen" signals.

STEPS/PROCESS: FINAL PAPER PREPARATION

  1. Community Identification: Define the discourse community you are writing for (e.g., Nursing, Engineering, Humanities).
  2. Drafting to Length: Aim for the 1500-word set point to ensure depth of argument.
  3. Tone Check: Review the paper to ensure tone doesn't shift into informal or overly conversational language.
  4. Editing: If you fall outside the 1450–1600 range, "kick on" the editing phase to either expand or condense the content.

RULES: THE RUBRIC MARGINS

  • The 1500 Rule: The final paper has a set point of 1500 words.
  • Wiggle Room: 1501.4 or similar minor decimals are within the normal range; do not stress over micro-fluctuations.
  • Red Pen Signals: These occur when you deviate too far from the rubric—either by being significantly under-length or by losing professional formality.
  • Final Format: The exam will be all Multiple Choice, whereas the Monday module may include short answer components.

EXAMPLE/DEMONSTRATION

1. Discourse Community Example

In a Medical Discourse Community, using "H2O" is expected; in a Creative Writing Community, saying "the clear, life-giving water" might be more appropriate.

2. Word Count Variance

If your draft is 1475 words, you are within the Normal Range. If it is 1200 words, you have left the range and must expand your "Synthesis Layer" to reach the set point.

3. Tone Consistency

Incorrect: "I think this is a cool idea."
Correct (Formal): "The evidence suggests this methodology is highly effective within the discipline."

PRACTICE PROBLEMS

  1. What is the targeted word count (set point) for the final paper? (Answer: 1500 words)
  2. If a student's paper is 1580 words, is it considered within the "normal range"? (Answer: Yes)
  3. The final exam format will be what? (Answer: All Multiple Choice)

SUMMARY

Final success in the course relies on staying within the Normal Range of academic expectations. By meeting the 1500-word set point and adhering to the tone requirements of your specific Discourse Community, you ensure your paper meets the professional standards required for the final grade.
