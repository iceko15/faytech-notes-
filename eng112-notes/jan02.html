Research Methodologies & Analysis

BIG IDEA

A professional research paper is a multi-layered construct. It requires a balance between Qualitative Research (interpretation and context) and strict adherence to Citation Logic to avoid plagiarism and ensure academic integrity.

KEY TERMS

Term Definition
Qualitative Research Research focused on interpreting meaning, context, and discourse (e.g., Ethnography).
Boolean Logic Search operators (AND, OR, NOT) used to refine database queries.
Literature Review A synthesized overview of existing research on a specific topic.
Abstract A concise summary of the paper, including keywords for database indexing.

STEPS/PROCESS: THE SEARCH TRANSFORMATION

  1. Horizontal Query Shift: Start with broad terms and use "OR" to expand your initial search radius.
  2. Vertical Refinement: Use "AND" to intersect terms and drill down into specific results.
  3. Evaluation: Determine if a source is a Primary Source or a secondary interpretation.
  4. Synthesis: Annotate sources to move from simple summary to an active argument layer.

RULES: ACADEMIC CITATION SYSTEMS

  • The "House" Analogy: Humanities typically use MLA (Modern Language Association), while Sciences use APA (American Psychological Association).
  • Direct Conversion: Always translate raw source data into the correct bibliographical format immediately to prevent plagiarism.
  • Works Cited / References: This is the "outer layer" of the paper—the first thing checked for academic validity.
  • Semantics vs. Syntax: Focus on the meaning (semantics) within the interpretation layer while maintaining structural rules (syntax) in the writing.

EXAMPLE/DEMONSTRATION

1. Boolean Query Strategy

To find specific papers on social media's effect on sleep:
(Social Media OR Instagram) AND (Sleep Deprivation)

2. Synthesis vs. Summary

Summary: "Author A says social media is bad."
Synthesis: "While Author A argues that social media is detrimental, Author B provides an ethnography showing that it can foster community, suggesting a more nuanced discourse."

3. The Abstract Layer

An Abstract should contain: The problem, the method (Qualitative), the findings, and the implications.

PRACTICE PROBLEMS

  1. Which search operator expands your results, "AND" or "OR"? (Answer: OR)
  2. Which citation style is most common in Humanities? (Answer: MLA)
  3. What is the name of the research method that involves studying people and cultures (Ethnography)? (Answer: Qualitative Research)

SUMMARY

Success in ENG-112 involves navigating the Argument Layer and the Synthesis Layer. By mastering Boolean logic and understanding the distinction between MLA and APA, you can transform a simple database query into a professional, academic 10-page research paper.
