ENG-112: Writing/Research in the Disciplines

Instructor: Anne Greenley | Date: January 14, 2026

BIG IDEA

Success in ENG-112 is built on transitioning from the general writing skills of ENG-111 to specialized research and writing within the Humanities, Social Sciences, and Natural Sciences. Understanding the specific structures, tones, and documentation styles (MLA vs. APA) of each field is the primary goal of the course.

KEY TERMS

Term Definition
IMRAD Standard structure for Science writing: Introduction, Methods, Results, and Discussion.
SLR Method A framework for analyzing writing across disciplines: Structure, Language, and Reference.
Qualitative Research based on words, stories, interviews, and quality of experience.
Quantitative Research based on quantity, numerical data, and statistical analysis \( (n = \text{sample size}) \).
Primary Source The original text or experiment (e.g., a novel or raw lab data).
Secondary Source Analysis or criticism of a primary source (e.g., a journal article about a novel).

THE THREE DISCIPLINES (SLR ANALYSIS)

1. Humanities

  • Structure: Thesis-driven, moving from general ideas to specific interpretations.
  • Language: Creative, figurative, assertive tone; active voice is common.
  • Reference: Exclusively uses MLA documentation.

2. Social Sciences

  • Structure: Structured around a hypothesis and data; uses headings/subheadings.
  • Language: Objective and cautious; minimizes bias; often uses passive voice.
  • Reference: Exclusively uses APA (American Psychological Association).

3. Natural Sciences

  • Structure: Follows the IMRAD format; focuses on reproducibility.
  • Language: Plain, precise, and highly detached; avoids personal terms/first person.
  • Reference: Uses APA (for this specific course) but varies in professional practice.

EXAMPLE: THE TROLLEY PROBLEM APPROACH

Humanities Approach: Focuses on the ethical and moral "human experience." You might write a poem or a narrative from the perspective of the "large man" or the person pulling the lever to explore the internal struggle of right vs. wrong.
Natural Sciences Approach: Focuses on methodologies and physical probability. You might analyze the velocity of the train or the engineering of the track switch: \[ F = ma \] (Force = Mass × Acceleration) to determine if a human body could actually stop a train.
Social Sciences Approach: Focuses on human behavior and societal patterns. You might conduct a "mock interview" with the train conductor or analyze the statistics of how many people choose "the greater good" vs. "doing no harm."

PRACTICE QUESTIONS

  1. In which discipline is an assertive, opinionated tone most acceptable? (Humanities)
  2. What is the primary documentation style used for Social and Natural Sciences in this class? (APA)
  3. True or False: Quantitative research relies on interviews and focus groups. (False - that is Qualitative)
  4. What is the minimum word count required for each disciplinary paragraph in the Trolley Problem assignment? (100 words per discipline)
