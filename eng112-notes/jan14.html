BIG IDEA

[cite_start]

English 112 is a significant advancement from English 111, focusing on writing and research within three specific academic disciplines: the Humanities, Social Sciences, and Natural Sciences[cite: 250, 291]. [cite_start]Students will learn to adapt their writing style, documentation, and methodology to meet the professional standards of each field[cite: 292, 295].

Term Definition
HumanitiesA field focusing on literature, philosophy, and art, typically using creative, assertive tones and MLA documentation[cite: 370, 378, 379].
Social SciencesThe study of human behavior and societies using objective analysis, APA documentation, and structural tools like hypotheses[cite: 374, 375, 379].

Humanities:

    [cite_start]
  • Structure: Thesis at the end of the first paragraph[cite: 377].
    • [cite_start]
  • Language: Active voice, assertive tone, and figurative language[cite: 378].
    • [cite_start]
  • Reference: MLA documentation[cite: 379].

Social Sciences:

    [cite_start]
  • Structure: Uses headers, subheadings, and hypotheses[cite: 377, 378].
    • [cite_start]
  • Language: Passive voice, cautious tone, and objective detachment[cite: 378, 379].
    • [cite_start]
  • Reference: APA documentation[cite: 379].

Natural Sciences:

    [cite_start]
  • Structure: Strict MRAD (Introduction, Methods, Results, and Discussion)[cite: 373, 378].
    • [cite_start]
  • Language: Precise, plain language; avoids personal pronouns like "I"[cite: 374, 378, 379].
    • [cite_start]
  • Reference: APA (for this class) or Council of Science Editors (CSE)[cite: 380, 381].
