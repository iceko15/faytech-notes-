Persuasion & Evidence Systems

BIG IDEA

Advanced persuasion relies on the synthesis of high-quality evidence (Peer-Reviewed sources) and the strategic use of Aristotle’s Appeals to bridge the gap between logical data and emotional audience connection.

KEY TERMS

Term Definition
Pathos The rhetorical appeal to emotion; the system that links logic to human feeling.
Active Listening The inverse of speaking; a critical feedback loop where the audience processes the message.
Peer-Reviewed The gold standard for reference sources, ensuring data has been vetted by experts.
Feedback Loop The sum of all demographics and audience reactions during a live speech.

STEPS/PROCESS: THE PERSUASION FLOW

  1. Synthesize Data: Combining various pieces of evidence into a cohesive narrative rather than listing facts.
  2. Tell-Show-Tell: State the point (Tell), provide the evidence/demonstration (Show), and reinforce the takeaway (Tell).
  3. Verbal Citation: Using a direct quote or credit to a source during the delivery to establish authority.
  4. Q&A Management: Preparing for the 180-second (3 minute) window of audience interrogation following the speech.
  5. Call to Action: The final phase of Monroe's Motivated Sequence where you tell the audience exactly what to do.

RULES: MONROE’S MOTIVATED SEQUENCE

  • Attention: Gain the audience's focus immediately.
  • Need: Demonstrate that a problem exists.
  • Satisfaction: Provide a solution to the problem.
  • Visualization: Show the audience the benefits of the solution.
  • Action: Provide a specific, low-friction next step for the audience.

EXAMPLE/DEMONSTRATION

1. The Verbal Citation: "According to a 2025 Peer-Reviewed study in the Journal of Communication, 70% of audience retention is based on the feedback loop."
2. Tell-Show-Tell in Action: (Tell) "Active listening is rare." (Show) "Look at how often people check phones during a 10-minute speech." (Tell) "Therefore, we must work harder to earn attention."
3. The Q&A Rule: Treat the 180 seconds of Q&A as an extension of the speech, not a separate informal chat.

PRACTICE PROBLEMS

  1. What is the most reliable type of reference source for a speech? (Answer: Peer-Reviewed)
  2. Monroe’s Motivated Sequence ends with which specific section? (Answer: Call to Action)
  3. What term describes the link between the logic system and the emotion system? (Answer: Pathos)

SUMMARY

Persuasion is more than just delivering data; it is the act of synthesizing evidence through Pathos and Ethos. By following the Tell-Show-Tell model and concluding with a clear Call to Action, a speaker can effectively close the feedback loop and move an audience to action.
