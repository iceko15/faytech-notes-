Introduction Speech & Course Overview

BIG IDEA

The first module of COM-231 focuses on mastering the Extemporaneous speaking style through a 2–4 minute "Object Speech." Instead of a standard biographical introduction, students use a physical or metaphorical object to represent their personality, experiences, and goals.

KEY TERMS

Term Definition
Extemporaneous A delivery style that is practiced and prepared but not memorized word-for-word; uses brief notecards for guidance.
Attention Grabber The opening of a speech designed to hook the audience (e.g., a story, joke, or provocative question).
Clincher The final thought of a speech that leaves a memorable impression on the audience.
Fillers Vocalized pauses like "um," "uh," or "like" that can distract from the message.

STEPS/PROCESS: GETTING STARTED

  1. Course Entry Quiz: Must be completed first to unlock all course modules.
  2. Module 1 Readings: Review "The Art of Public Speaking" by Lucas and Staab. Note that e-book reading links show 150 points but do NOT count toward the final grade.
  3. Chapter Quizzes: These are open-book, open-note, untimed, and allow two attempts. Look for the rocket ship icon.
  4. The Outline: Download the template and write the speech in complete sentences. Do not use essay/paragraph form.

RULES: SPEECH DELIVERY & RECORDING

  • Time Limit: Speeches must be between 2 and 4 minutes long.
  • Audience: You must have at least 3 adult/teenage audience members present. Show them on camera at the start.
  • The Take: The recording must be one continuous, unedited take. No splicing or cutting.
  • Posture: You must be standing (unless medically exempt). Do not sit at a desk.
  • Visuals: Use notecards and maintain eye contact with the camera/audience. Avoid reading off a screen.

EXAMPLE: THE OBJECT COMPARISON

Object: Aloe Plant

Comparison 1: Resilience – Grows in harsh conditions (personal perseverance).

Comparison 2: Healing Properties – Used for medicine (career goal of becoming a nurse).

Object: Childhood Blanket

Comparison 1: Durability – It has holes and stains but still functions (life experiences).

Comparison 2: Comfort – Provides security to others (role in family/friendships).

PRACTICE/SELF-CHECK

  • Does my introduction include an attention grabber AND a preview of my main points? (Yes/No)
  • Are my transitions written between each main point in the body? (Yes/No)
  • Did I verify that my YouTube link is "Unlisted" rather than "Private"? (Yes/No)
  • Did I record in a way that shows my body language and hand motions? (Yes/No)

SUMMARY

Success in this module requires early completion of the entry quiz, diligent outlining in complete sentences, and a recorded delivery that feels conversational and energetic. Focus on the details of your object comparison rather than the object itself to ensure your audience learns specific facts about your life and goals.
