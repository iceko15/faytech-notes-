Public Speaking (COM-231): Course Introduction

BIG IDEA

This course focuses on the development of professional communication skills through structured speech delivery, audience analysis, and the synthesis of research into persuasive and informative presentations.

KEY TERMS

Term Definition
Course Entry Quiz A mandatory Canvas requirement for hybrid courses to officially record enrollment.
Audience Analysis The process of gathering and interpreting information about recipients of oral communication to improve effectiveness.
Extemporaneous Delivery A speech style that is practiced and prepared but delivered using brief notes rather than a full script or memorization.
Rubric A scoring guide used to evaluate the quality of a student's speech based on specific criteria like eye contact and evidence.

STEPS/PROCESS: GETTING STARTED

  1. Log into Canvas: Ensure access to the COM-231 section 4 module.
  2. Complete Course Entry Quiz: Must be completed before Wednesday; consists of 5 true/false questions regarding the syllabus.
  3. Review Prerequisites: Confirm completion of English 025 or equivalent basic writing requirements.
  4. Prepare for Speeches: Anticipate four graded speeches over the 16-week semester (roughly one per month).

RULES FOR EFFECTIVE DELIVERY

  • Avoid memorizing speeches word-for-word; it often leads to catastrophic failure if a line is forgotten.
  • Use note cards effectively: aim for one main point per card.
  • Maintain a conversational tone and prioritize looking at the audience over looking at notes.
  • Incorporate research early: The final speech requires at least 7 cited sources.
  • Cite sources verbally during the speech (e.g., "According to...") to avoid plagiarism.

EXAMPLE/DEMONSTRATION: SPEECH STRUCTURE

The Hook: An opening statement designed to grab the audience's attention immediately.

Credibility Statement: Briefly explaining why you are qualified to speak on the topic or the research you have conducted.

Preview: A clear outline provided to the audience of the main points the speech will cover.

PRACTICE REFLECTION

  1. How many unique sources are required for the final speech? (7 sources)
  2. What is the recommended format for notes during a speech? (Note cards with one point per card)
  3. Why is "conviction" important in topic selection? (If the speaker is bored, the audience will be bored; interest drives delivery)

SUMMARY

The first session established the logistical requirements for COM-231, specifically the Canvas entry quiz and the hybrid nature of the course. Dr. Edwards emphasized that successful public speaking is developmental—grading becomes stricter as the semester progresses and more techniques (delivery, research, and organization) are taught. Key focus areas include overcoming anxiety through audience connection and adhering to the structured rubric checklists.
