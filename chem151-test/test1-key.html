Topics: Matter, Atoms, Naming, Stoichiometry (Ch 1-3)

1. Which of the following is a chemical change?

  • A) Boiling water
  • B) Crushing a soda can
  • C) Rusting of iron
  • D) Dissolving sugar in water
  • E) Melting ice
Solution: Rusting creates a new substance (Iron Oxide). The others are physical changes.

2. Perform the calculation with correct significant figures: (2.50 × 102) ÷ 5.0

  • A) 50
  • B) 50.
  • C) 5.0 × 101
  • D) 50.0
  • E) 50.00
Solution: 250 / 5.0 = 50. The limiting term (5.0) has 2 sig figs. The answer must have 2 sig figs. 5.0 × 101 is the clearest way to show 2 sig figs.

3. An atom with 26 protons, 30 neutrons, and 23 electrons is an ion of:

  • A) Zn2+
  • B) Fe3+
  • C) Fe2+
  • D) Ni3+
  • E) Mn2+
Solution: 26 protons = Iron (Fe). Charge = Protons - Electrons = 26 - 23 = +3.

4. What is the correct name for Cu(NO3)2?

  • A) Copper dinitrate
  • B) Copper(I) nitrate
  • C) Copper(II) nitrate
  • D) Copper nitrogen oxide
  • E) Cuprous nitrate
Solution: Nitrate is NO3-. Since there are two nitrates, Cu must be +2.

5. What is the correct formula for diphosphorus pentoxide?

  • A) PO5
  • B) P2O5
  • C) P5O2
  • D) P2O4
  • E) (PO)5
Solution: Di = 2, Penta = 5. Covalent naming uses prefixes directly.

6. Naturally occurring lithium consists of two isotopes: 6Li (6.015 amu) and 7Li (7.016 amu). If the average atomic mass is 6.941 amu, which isotope is more abundant?

  • A) 6Li
  • B) 7Li
  • C) They are equal abundance
  • D) Cannot be determined
  • E) Neither
Solution: The average (6.941) is much closer to 7 than to 6, meaning 7Li is more abundant.

7. Calculate the molar mass of Calcium Phosphate, Ca3(PO4)2.

  • A) 279.21 g/mol
  • B) 310.18 g/mol
  • C) 135.05 g/mol
  • D) 215.21 g/mol
  • E) 430.42 g/mol
Solution: (3×40.08) + (2×30.97) + (8×16.00) = 310.18 g/mol.

8. How many moles of CO2 are present in 88.0 g of CO2?

  • A) 0.500 mol
  • B) 1.00 mol
  • C) 2.00 mol
  • D) 4.00 mol
  • E) 44.0 mol
Solution: Molar mass CO2 = 44.01 g/mol. 88.0 g / 44.01 g/mol ≈ 2.00 mol.

9. Which of the following is an alkali metal?

  • A) Mg
  • B) Al
  • C) K
  • D) Cl
  • E) Ar
Solution: Group 1A elements (Li, Na, K, etc.) are Alkali Metals.

10. What is the percent composition of Carbon in C2H6?

  • A) 20.1%
  • B) 25.0%
  • C) 74.9%
  • D) 79.9%
  • E) 92.3%
Solution: Mass C = 24.02. Total Mass = 30.07. (24.02 / 30.07) × 100 = 79.9%.

11. Balance the following equation: __ C3H8 + __ O2 → __ CO2 + __ H2O

  • A) 1, 3, 3, 4
  • B) 1, 5, 3, 4
  • C) 2, 10, 6, 8
  • D) 1, 4, 3, 4
  • E) 1, 5, 3, 2
Solution: 3 Carbons → 3 CO2. 8 Hydrogens → 4 H2O. Total Oxygens = (3×2) + (4×1) = 10. Need 5 O2.

12. What is the correct name for the acid H2SO3?

  • A) Sulfuric acid
  • B) Hydrosulfuric acid
  • C) Sulfurous acid
  • D) Persulfuric acid
  • E) Hyposulfurous acid
Solution: SO32- is Sulfite. "ite" becomes "ous". Sulfurous acid.

13. A compound has the empirical formula CH2O and a molar mass of 180 g/mol. What is the molecular formula?

  • A) CH2O
  • B) C2H4O2
  • C) C6H12O6
  • D) C3H6O3
  • E) C5H10O5
Solution: Mass of CH2O = 30 g/mol. 180 / 30 = 6. Multiply subscripts by 6.

14. How many atoms are in 0.500 moles of Gold (Au)?

  • A) 3.01 × 1023
  • B) 6.02 × 1023
  • C) 1.20 × 1024
  • D) 197
  • E) 98.5
Solution: 0.500 mol × (6.022 × 1023 atoms/mol) = 3.01 × 1023.

15. In the reaction 2 H2 + O2 → 2 H2O, if you start with 4.0 moles of H2 and 3.0 moles of O2, what is the limiting reactant?

  • A) H2
  • B) O2
  • C) H2O
  • D) Both are limiting
  • E) No reaction occurs
Solution: 4.0 mol H2 requires 2.0 mol O2. We have 3.0 mol O2 (excess). H2 runs out first.

16. Convert 25.0 °C to Kelvin.

  • A) 57.0 K
  • B) 248.15 K
  • C) 298.15 K
  • D) 100.0 K
  • E) 212.0 K
Solution: K = °C + 273.15.

17. Which pair of elements would likely form an ionic compound?

  • A) N and O
  • B) C and H
  • C) Na and Cl
  • D) Si and O
  • E) S and Cl
Solution: Ionic = Metal + Nonmetal. Na is a metal, Cl is a nonmetal.

18. If the theoretical yield of a reaction is 50.0 g and the actual yield is 45.0 g, what is the percent yield?

  • A) 111%
  • B) 90.0%
  • C) 50.0%
  • D) 45.0%
  • E) 10.0%
Solution: (Actual / Theoretical) × 100 = (45.0 / 50.0) × 100 = 90.0%.

19. What is the density of an object with a mass of 15.0 g and a volume of 3.0 mL?

  • A) 0.20 g/mL
  • B) 5.0 g/mL
  • C) 45 g/mL
  • D) 12 g/mL
  • E) 18 g/mL
Solution: Density = Mass / Volume = 15.0 / 3.0 = 5.0 g/mL.

22. Stoichiometry:

Molar Mass Fe2O3 = 159.7 g/mol. Molar Mass Fe = 55.85 g/mol.

150.0 g Fe2O3 × (1 mol / 159.7 g) × (2 mol Fe / 1 mol Fe2O3) × (55.85 g Fe / 1 mol Fe) = 104.9 g Fe

23. Empirical Formula:

Assume 100g sample:

  • C: 40.0g / 12.01 = 3.33 mol
  • H: 6.7g / 1.008 = 6.65 mol
  • O: 53.3g / 16.00 = 3.33 mol

Divide by smallest (3.33): C=1, H=2, O=1. Formula: CH2O

24. Dimensional Analysis:

3.00 × 108 m/s × (1 mi / 1609 m) × (3600 s / 1 hr) = 6.71 × 108 mph

25. Limiting Reactant:

N2: 28.0g / 28.02 g/mol = 1.00 mol. Produces 2.00 mol NH3.

H2: 6.0g / 2.016 g/mol = 2.98 mol. Produces (2.98 × 2/3) = 1.99 mol NH3.

H2 produces less, so H2 is limiting.

Yield: 1.99 mol NH3 × 17.03 g/mol = 33.9 g NH3.
