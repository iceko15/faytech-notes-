Topics: Electronic Structure of Atoms (Ch 6)

1. What is the frequency of light with a wavelength of 500 nm? (c = 3.00 x 108 m/s)

  • A) 6.00 x 1014 Hz
  • B) 1.50 x 102 Hz
  • C) 6.00 x 105 Hz
  • D) 1.67 x 10-15 Hz
  • E) 5.00 x 1014 Hz
Solution: ν = c / λ. 500 nm = 5.00 x 10-7 m. ν = 3.00 x 108 / 5.00 x 10-7 = 6.00 x 1014 Hz.

2. Which quantum number describes the shape of an orbital?

  • A) Principal (n)
  • B) Angular Momentum (l)
  • C) Magnetic (ml)
  • D) Spin (ms)
  • E) None of the above
Solution: l determines shape (s, p, d, f). n determines size/energy. ml determines orientation.

3. How many orbitals are in the 3d subshell?

  • A) 1
  • B) 3
  • C) 5
  • D) 7
  • E) 9
Solution: d subshell corresponds to l=2. ml values are -2, -1, 0, 1, 2 (5 orbitals).

4. What is the maximum number of electrons that can occupy the n = 2 shell?

  • A) 2
  • B) 4
  • C) 6
  • D) 8
  • E) 18
Solution: Max electrons = 2n2. 2(2)2 = 8. (2s2 + 2p6).

5. Which electron configuration represents the ground state of Nitrogen?

  • A) 1s2 2s2 2p3
  • B) 1s2 2s2 2p4
  • C) 1s2 2s1 2p4
  • D) 1s2 2p5
  • E) [He] 2s2 2p2
Solution: Nitrogen has 7 electrons. 2 in 1s, 2 in 2s, 3 in 2p.

6. The energy of a photon is directly proportional to its:

  • A) Wavelength
  • B) Frequency
  • C) Amplitude
  • D) Speed
  • E) Mass
Solution: E = hν. Energy is proportional to frequency.

7. Which of the following transitions in a hydrogen atom emits the highest energy photon?

  • A) n = 2 → n = 1
  • B) n = 3 → n = 2
  • C) n = 4 → n = 3
  • D) n = 5 → n = 4
  • E) n = 6 → n = 5
Solution: Energy gaps decrease as n increases. The n=2 to n=1 gap is the largest.

8. Which element has the electron configuration [Ar] 4s2 3d10 4p3?

  • A) As
  • B) Ge
  • C) Se
  • D) Sb
  • E) P
Solution: Count electrons: 18 (Ar) + 2 + 10 + 3 = 33. Atomic number 33 is Arsenic (As).

9. The Pauli Exclusion Principle states that:

  • A) Electrons fill lowest energy levels first
  • B) No two electrons can have the same 4 quantum numbers
  • C) Electrons fill degenerate orbitals singly first
  • D) It is impossible to know position and momentum simultaneously
  • E) Energy is quantized
Solution: This implies an orbital can hold max 2 electrons with opposite spins.

10. Which orbital is spherical in shape?

  • A) s
  • B) p
  • C) d
  • D) f
  • E) g
Solution: s orbitals are spherical. p are dumbbell shaped.

11. Calculate the energy of a photon with frequency 4.00 x 1014 Hz. (h = 6.626 x 10-34 J·s)

  • A) 2.65 x 10-19 J
  • B) 1.66 x 10-48 J
  • C) 6.03 x 1047 J
  • D) 2.65 x 10-40 J
  • E) 1.50 x 10-19 J
Solution: E = hν = (6.626 x 10-34)(4.00 x 1014) = 2.65 x 10-19 J.

12. Which set of quantum numbers is invalid?

  • A) n=1, l=0, ml=0, ms=+1/2
  • B) n=2, l=1, ml=-1, ms=-1/2
  • C) n=3, l=2, ml=-2, ms=+1/2
  • D) n=2, l=2, ml=0, ms=-1/2
  • E) n=4, l=3, ml=3, ms=+1/2
Solution: l must be less than n. If n=2, l can only be 0 or 1. l=2 is impossible.

13. Hund's Rule relates to:

  • A) Pairing electrons in orbitals
  • B) The uncertainty of electron position
  • C) The wave nature of matter
  • D) The photoelectric effect
  • E) Nuclear shielding
Solution: Hund's Rule states that for degenerate orbitals, electrons fill singly with parallel spins before pairing.

14. What is the wavelength of a photon with energy 3.00 x 10-19 J?

  • A) 663 nm
  • B) 450 nm
  • C) 500 nm
  • D) 200 nm
  • E) 1000 nm
Solution: E = hc/λ → λ = hc/E. λ = (6.626e-34 * 3e8) / 3e-19 = 6.63e-7 m = 663 nm.

15. Which ion is isoelectronic with Neon?

  • A) O2-
  • B) F-
  • C) Na+
  • D) Mg2+
  • E) All of the above
Solution: Neon has 10 electrons. O2- (8+2), F- (9+1), Na+ (11-1), Mg2+ (12-2) all have 10 electrons.

20. Periodic Trends:

Nitrogen (2p3) has a half-filled p-subshell, which is relatively stable. Oxygen (2p4) has one paired electron in the p-subshell. The electron-electron repulsion from this pair makes it easier to remove one electron from Oxygen than from the stable half-filled shell of Nitrogen.
