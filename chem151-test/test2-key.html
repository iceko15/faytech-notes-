KEY: Practice Test 2

Topics: Matter, Atoms, Naming, Stoichiometry (Ch 1-3)

1. Which of the following is an intensive physical property?

  • A) Mass
  • B) Volume
  • C) Density
  • D) Length
  • E) Heat Content
Solution: Intensive properties do not depend on the amount of matter. Density is constant regardless of sample size.

2. How many significant figures are in the number 0.004050?

  • A) 3
  • B) 4
  • C) 5
  • D) 6
  • E) 7
Solution: Leading zeros are not significant. Trailing zeros after a decimal are significant. Digits: 4, 0, 5, 0.

3. Which element is a halogen?

  • A) Neon
  • B) Oxygen
  • C) Calcium
  • D) Bromine
  • E) Iron
Solution: Halogens are Group 7A (F, Cl, Br, I).

4. What is the correct formula for Aluminum Sulfide?

  • A) AlS
  • B) Al2S3
  • C) Al3S2
  • D) AlSO4
  • E) Al2(SO3)3
Solution: Al is +3, S is -2. Cross charges: Al2S3.

5. Name the compound SF6.

  • A) Sulfur fluoride
  • B) Sulfur hexafluoride
  • C) Monosulfur heptafluoride
  • D) Sulfur(VI) fluoride
  • E) Silicate fluoride
Solution: Covalent compound. Hexa = 6.

6. How many neutrons are in the isotope 37Cl?

  • A) 17
  • B) 20
  • C) 37
  • D) 35.45
  • E) 18
Solution: Mass Number - Protons = Neutrons. 37 - 17 (Atomic # of Cl) = 20.

7. Calculate the mass of 0.25 moles of CH4.

  • A) 16.0 g
  • B) 4.0 g
  • C) 64.0 g
  • D) 3.0 g
  • E) 0.015 g
Solution: Molar mass CH4 = 16.04 g/mol. 0.25 mol × 16.04 g/mol ≈ 4.0 g.

8. Which of the following contains the greatest number of atoms?

  • A) 1 mole of He
  • B) 1 mole of H2
  • C) 1 mole of CH4
  • D) 1 mole of H2O
  • E) They all contain the same number of atoms
Solution: 1 mole of molecules is the same, but atoms differ. CH4 has 5 atoms per molecule. He=1, H2=2, H2O=3.

9. What is the charge of the cation in Fe2O3?

  • A) +1
  • B) +2
  • C) +3
  • D) -2
  • E) 0
Solution: Oxygen is -2. 3(-2) = -6. Fe2 must be +6. So Fe is +3.

10. What is the empirical formula of C4H8O2?

  • A) C4H8O2
  • B) C2H4O
  • C) CH2O
  • D) C2H2O
  • E) CHO
Solution: Divide subscripts by the greatest common factor (2).

11. Balance the equation: Al + O2 → Al2O3

  • A) Al + O2 → Al2O3
  • B) 2 Al + 3 O2 → 2 Al2O3
  • C) 4 Al + 3 O2 → 2 Al2O3
  • D) 3 Al + 2 O2 → Al3O4
  • E) 2 Al + 1.5 O2 → Al2O3
Solution: To balance Oxygen (2 and 3), use 6. 3 O2 and 2 Al2O3. This requires 4 Al.

12. Name the acid HClO4.

  • A) Chloric acid
  • B) Hydrochloric acid
  • C) Perchloric acid
  • D) Hypochlorous acid
  • E) Chlorous acid
Solution: ClO4- is Perchlorate. "ate" -> "ic". Perchloric acid.

13. A mixture of sand and water is best separated by:

  • A) Distillation
  • B) Filtration
  • C) Chromatography
  • D) Electrolysis
  • E) Combustion
Solution: Filtration separates a solid (sand) from a liquid (water).

14. What is the mass percent of Oxygen in H2O?

  • A) 11.2%
  • B) 33.3%
  • C) 66.6%
  • D) 88.8%
  • E) 100%
Solution: Mass O = 16.00. Total = 18.02. (16.00/18.02)*100 = 88.8%.

15. In the reaction N2 + 3 H2 → 2 NH3, how many moles of NH3 are produced from 6 moles of H2 (assuming excess N2)?

  • A) 2 moles
  • B) 3 moles
  • C) 4 moles
  • D) 6 moles
  • E) 9 moles
Solution: Ratio is 3 H2 : 2 NH3. If H2 doubles (3 to 6), NH3 doubles (2 to 4).

16. Which of the following is a molecular (covalent) compound?

  • A) NaCl
  • B) KBr
  • C) CO2
  • D) MgCl2
  • E) FeO
Solution: CO2 is Nonmetal + Nonmetal. The others are ionic.

17. Convert 500 mg to grams.

  • A) 500,000 g
  • B) 50 g
  • C) 5.0 g
  • D) 0.5 g
  • E) 0.05 g
Solution: 1 g = 1000 mg. 500 / 1000 = 0.5 g.

18. Rutherford's Gold Foil experiment demonstrated the existence of:

  • A) Electrons
  • B) The nucleus
  • C) Neutrons
  • D) Isotopes
  • E) Energy levels
Solution: The deflection of alpha particles proved a dense, positive nucleus.

19. If a reaction has a percent yield of 80% and the theoretical yield is 10.0 g, what is the actual yield?

  • A) 8.0 g
  • B) 10.0 g
  • C) 12.5 g
  • D) 2.0 g
  • E) 80.0 g
Solution: Actual = Theoretical × % = 10.0 × 0.80 = 8.0 g.

20. Which ion is isoelectronic with Argon?

  • A) Na+
  • B) F-
  • C) Ca2+
  • D) O2-
  • E) Ne
Solution: Argon has 18 electrons. Ca has 20 protons; Ca2+ has 18 electrons.
