21. A piece of wood has a mass of 12.5 g and a volume of 15.0 cm3. Calculate its density in g/cm3.
Answer: 0.833 g/cm3
Density = mass / volume = 12.5 g / 15.0 cm3 = 0.8333... g/cm3. (3 sig figs)
22. Balance the combustion reaction of butane: _C4H10 + _O2 → _CO2 + _H2O.
Answer: 2, 13, 8, 10
2C4H10 + 13O2 → 8CO2 + 10H2O
23. How many grams of oxygen are required to completely react with 12.0 g of carbon to form carbon dioxide? C + O2 → CO2.
Answer: 32.0 g
12.0 g C * (1 mol C / 12.01 g) ≈ 1 mol C.
1 mol C needs 1 mol O2. 1 mol O2 is 32.0 g.
24. A compound contains 52.14% C, 13.13% H, and 34.73% O. Determine its empirical formula.
Answer: C2H6O
C: 52.14/12.01=4.34. H: 13.13/1.01=13.0. O: 34.73/16.0=2.17.
Divide by 2.17: C=2, H=6, O=1.
25. If 20.0 g of magnesium (Mg) reacts with excess oxygen (O2) to form magnesium oxide (MgO), what is the theoretical yield of MgO in grams? 2Mg + O2 → 2MgO.
Answer: 33.2 g
20.0 g Mg * (1 mol Mg / 24.31g) * (2 mol MgO / 2 mol Mg) * (40.31 g MgO / 1 mol MgO) = 33.16 g.
