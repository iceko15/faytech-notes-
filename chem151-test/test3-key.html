KEY: Practice Test 3

Topics: Aqueous Reactions, Solution Stoichiometry (Ch 4)

1. Which of the following compounds is insoluble in water?

  • A) NaNO3
  • B) K2SO4
  • C) AgCl
  • D) NH4Cl
  • E) LiBr
Solution: Chlorides are generally soluble, except with Ag+, Pb2+, and Hg22+.

2. What is the oxidation number of Sulfur in H2SO4?

  • A) +2
  • B) +4
  • C) +6
  • D) -2
  • E) 0
Solution: H is +1 (total +2), O is -2 (total -8). S must be +6 to balance.

3. Identify the spectator ions in the reaction: AgNO3(aq) + NaCl(aq) → AgCl(s) + NaNO3(aq)

  • A) Ag+ and Cl-
  • B) Na+ and NO3-
  • C) Ag+ and NO3-
  • D) Na+ and Cl-
  • E) There are no spectator ions
Solution: Spectator ions appear unchanged on both sides. Na+ and NO3- remain aqueous.

4. Which of the following is a strong acid?

  • A) HF
  • B) HC2H3O2
  • C) HNO2
  • D) HCl
  • E) H2CO3
Solution: HCl is one of the 7 strong acids. The others listed are weak acids.

5. Calculate the molarity of a solution prepared by dissolving 10.0 g of NaOH in enough water to make 250.0 mL of solution.

  • A) 0.250 M
  • B) 0.500 M
  • C) 1.00 M
  • D) 2.50 M
  • E) 4.00 M
Solution: Molar mass NaOH = 40.0 g/mol. 10.0 g / 40.0 g/mol = 0.250 mol. 0.250 mol / 0.250 L = 1.00 M.

6. In a redox reaction, the substance that is oxidized:

  • A) Gains electrons
  • B) Loses electrons
  • C) Decreases in oxidation number
  • D) Is the oxidizing agent
  • E) Becomes more negative
Solution: Oxidation is loss of electrons (OIL RIG).

7. What volume of 0.200 M HCl is required to neutralize 25.0 mL of 0.100 M NaOH?

  • A) 12.5 mL
  • B) 25.0 mL
  • C) 50.0 mL
  • D) 5.00 mL
  • E) 100.0 mL
Solution: M1V1 = M2V2 (1:1 ratio). (0.200)V = (0.100)(25.0). V = 12.5 mL.

8. Which of the following is a nonelectrolyte?

  • A) NaCl
  • B) HCl
  • C) C12H22O11 (Sucrose)
  • D) NaOH
  • E) KBr
Solution: Sucrose is a molecular compound that does not ionize in water.

9. What is the net ionic equation for the reaction between Hydrochloric acid and Sodium hydroxide?

  • A) HCl + NaOH → NaCl + H2O
  • B) H+ + Cl- + Na+ + OH- → Na+ + Cl- + H2O
  • C) H+ + OH- → H2O
  • D) Na+ + Cl- → NaCl
  • E) H+ + NaOH → Na+ + H2O
Solution: Strong acid + Strong base → Water. Spectator ions (Na+, Cl-) cancel out.

10. Which reaction is a precipitation reaction?

  • A) HCl(aq) + NaOH(aq) → NaCl(aq) + H2O(l)
  • B) 2 H2(g) + O2(g) → 2 H2O(g)
  • C) Pb(NO3)2(aq) + 2 KI(aq) → PbI2(s) + 2 KNO3(aq)
  • D) CH4(g) + 2 O2(g) → CO2(g) + 2 H2O(g)
  • E) CaCO3(s) → CaO(s) + CO2(g)
Solution: Reaction C forms a solid precipitate (PbI2) from aqueous reactants.

11. What is the concentration of nitrate ions in a 0.50 M solution of Mg(NO3)2?

  • A) 0.25 M
  • B) 0.50 M
  • C) 1.00 M
  • D) 1.50 M
  • E) 2.00 M
Solution: Each formula unit produces 2 nitrate ions. 0.50 M × 2 = 1.00 M.

12. Which element is reduced in the reaction: Zn(s) + Cu2+(aq) → Zn2+(aq) + Cu(s)?

  • A) Zn
  • B) Cu2+
  • C) Zn2+
  • D) Cu
  • E) None
Solution: Cu2+ gains electrons to become Cu(s). Reduction is gain.

13. How many grams of NaCl are needed to prepare 500.0 mL of a 0.150 M solution?

  • A) 4.38 g
  • B) 8.77 g
  • C) 2.92 g
  • D) 58.44 g
  • E) 0.075 g
Solution: 0.500 L × 0.150 mol/L = 0.075 mol. 0.075 mol × 58.44 g/mol = 4.38 g.

14. Which of the following is a weak electrolyte?

  • A) HCl
  • B) NaOH
  • C) NaCl
  • D) HC2H3O2 (Acetic Acid)
  • E) KNO3
Solution: Acetic acid is a weak acid and thus a weak electrolyte. The others are strong electrolytes.

15. What is the oxidation state of Nitrogen in NO3-?

  • A) +3
  • B) +5
  • C) -3
  • D) +2
  • E) +4
Solution: O is -2. 3(-2) = -6. Total charge is -1. N + (-6) = -1 → N = +5.

Complete Ionic: Pb2+ + 2NO3- + 2K+ + 2I- → PbI2(s) + 2K+ + 2NO3-

Net Ionic: Pb2+(aq) + 2 I-(aq) → PbI2(s)

17. Titration:

Reaction: H2SO4 + 2 NaOH → Na2SO4 + 2 H2O

Moles NaOH = 0.03450 L × 0.125 M = 0.0043125 mol

Moles H2SO4 = 0.0043125 mol NaOH × (1 mol H2SO4 / 2 mol NaOH) = 0.00215625 mol

Molarity = 0.00215625 mol / 0.02500 L = 0.0863 M

18. Redox Balancing:

Oxidation: Fe2+ → Fe3+ + e- (Multiply by 5)

Reduction: MnO4- + 8H+ + 5e- → Mn2+ + 4H2O

Balanced: MnO4- + 5Fe2+ + 8H+ → Mn2+ + 5Fe3+ + 4H2O

19. Dilution:

Use M1V1 = M2V2

(12.0 M)(V1) = (0.100 M)(500.0 mL)

V1 = 4.17 mL

Procedure: Measure 4.17 mL of 12.0 M HCl and add it to a volumetric flask containing some water, then dilute to the 500.0 mL mark with water.

20. Solution Stoichiometry:

Reaction: 2 AgNO3 + CaCl2 → 2 AgCl + Ca(NO3)2

Moles CaCl2 = 0.0150 L × 0.100 M = 0.00150 mol

Moles AgNO3 needed = 0.00150 mol CaCl2 × (2 mol AgNO3 / 1 mol CaCl2) = 0.00300 mol

Volume AgNO3 = 0.00300 mol / 0.250 M = 0.0120 L = 12.0 mL
