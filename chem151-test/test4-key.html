KEY: Practice Test 4

Topics: Thermochemistry (Ch 5)

1. Which of the following is an endothermic process?

  • A) Freezing water
  • B) Condensing steam
  • C) Burning wood
  • D) Melting ice
  • E) Cooling hot soup
Solution: Melting requires heat absorption (endothermic). The others release heat.

2. Calculate the kinetic energy of a 150 g baseball traveling at 40.0 m/s.

  • A) 120 J
  • B) 3000 J
  • C) 120,000 J
  • D) 60 J
  • E) 240 J
Solution: KE = 0.5 * m * v2. m = 0.150 kg. KE = 0.5 * 0.150 * (40.0)2 = 120 J.

3. The First Law of Thermodynamics states that:

  • A) Entropy always increases
  • B) Energy is neither created nor destroyed
  • C) Heat flows from cold to hot
  • D) Absolute zero cannot be reached
  • E) Enthalpy is constant
Solution: Conservation of energy.

4. If a system absorbs 50 J of heat and does 30 J of work on the surroundings, what is the change in internal energy (ΔE)?

  • A) +80 J
  • B) +20 J
  • C) -20 J
  • D) -80 J
  • E) +50 J
Solution: ΔE = q + w. q = +50 J (absorbed). w = -30 J (work done by system). ΔE = 50 - 30 = +20 J.

5. Which of the following has a standard enthalpy of formation (ΔH°f) of zero?

  • A) H2O(l)
  • B) O(g)
  • C) O2(g)
  • D) CO2(g)
  • E) NaCl(s)
Solution: Elements in their standard state (O2 gas) have ΔH°f = 0.

6. Calculate the heat required to raise the temperature of 50.0 g of water from 25.0°C to 75.0°C. (Specific heat of water = 4.184 J/g·°C)

  • A) 209 J
  • B) 10.5 kJ
  • C) 15.7 kJ
  • D) 5.23 kJ
  • E) 1.05 kJ
Solution: q = mCΔT = 50.0 * 4.184 * (75.0-25.0) = 10460 J = 10.5 kJ.

7. A reaction where ΔH is negative is called:

  • A) Endothermic
  • B) Exothermic
  • C) Endergonic
  • D) Isothermic
  • E) Adiabatic
Solution: Negative ΔH means heat is released (Exothermic).

8. Given: 2 A → B (ΔH = -50 kJ). What is ΔH for the reaction: B → 2 A?

  • A) -50 kJ
  • B) -100 kJ
  • C) +50 kJ
  • D) +25 kJ
  • E) +100 kJ
Solution: Reversing the reaction flips the sign of ΔH.

9. Which term represents the heat content of a system at constant pressure?

  • A) Entropy
  • B) Internal Energy
  • C) Work
  • D) Enthalpy
  • E) Temperature
Solution: Enthalpy (H) is defined as heat flow at constant pressure.

10. In a bomb calorimeter, reactions are carried out at constant:

  • A) Pressure
  • B) Volume
  • C) Temperature
  • D) Enthalpy
  • E) Entropy
Solution: Bomb calorimeters are rigid containers (constant volume).

11. Calculate the work done when a gas expands from 2.0 L to 5.0 L against a constant pressure of 1.5 atm. (1 L·atm = 101.3 J)

  • A) -4.5 J
  • B) +4.5 J
  • C) -456 J
  • D) +456 J
  • E) -304 J
Solution: w = -PΔV = -1.5 * (5.0 - 2.0) = -4.5 L·atm. -4.5 * 101.3 = -456 J.

12. The specific heat capacity of Al is 0.90 J/g·°C. How much heat is released when 10.0 g of Al cools by 10.0°C?

  • A) 9.0 J
  • B) 90.0 J
  • C) 0.90 J
  • D) 81.0 J
  • E) 100 J
Solution: q = mCΔT = 10.0 * 0.90 * 10.0 = 90.0 J.

13. Which of the following is a state function?

  • A) Heat (q)
  • B) Work (w)
  • C) Enthalpy (H)
  • D) Distance traveled
  • E) Time
Solution: Enthalpy depends only on the current state, not the path taken. Heat and work are path functions.

14. Standard conditions for thermodynamics are:

  • A) 0 atm, 0°C
  • B) 1 atm, 0°C
  • C) 1 atm, 25°C
  • D) 1 atm, 100°C
  • E) 0 atm, 25°C
Solution: Standard state is 1 atm and typically 25°C (298 K).

15. If the enthalpy of combustion of methane is -890 kJ/mol, how much heat is released when 2.0 moles of methane are burned?

  • A) 445 kJ
  • B) 890 kJ
  • C) 1780 kJ
  • D) 2670 kJ
  • E) 0 kJ
Solution: -890 kJ/mol * 2.0 mol = -1780 kJ. (1780 kJ released).
