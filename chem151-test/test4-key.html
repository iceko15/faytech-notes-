21. Convert 25.0 °F to °C. The conversion formula is °C = (°F - 32) * 5/9.
Answer: -3.9 °C
°C = (25.0 - 32) * 5/9 = -7 * 5/9 = -3.88... °C.
22. How many grams of solute are needed to prepare 250. mL of a 0.500 M NaCl solution?
Answer: 7.31 g
Moles = M * L = 0.500 * 0.250 = 0.125 mol.
Grams = 0.125 mol * 58.44 g/mol = 7.305 g.
23. For the reaction 2H2 + O2 → 2H2O, how many grams of H2O are formed when 4.0 g of H2 react completely?
Answer: 36 g
4.0 g H2 * (1 mol H2 / 2.02 g) * (2 mol H2O / 2 mol H2) * (18.02 g H2O / 1 mol H2O) = 35.68 g.
24. A 10.0 g sample of a compound contains 2.01 g of hydrogen, 32.69 g of sulfur, and the rest is oxygen. What is the empirical formula?
Answer: H2SO4
Mass O = 100 - 2.01 - 32.69 = 65.3 g. Assume 100g sample. H: 2.01/1.01=1.99. S: 32.69/32.07=1.02. O: 65.3/16=4.08. Divide by 1.02: H=2, S=1, O=4.
25. If 15.0 g of N2 reacts with 15.0 g of H2 to form NH3, what is the maximum amount of NH3 (in grams) that can be produced? N2 + 3H2 → 2NH3.
Answer: 18.2 g
Moles N2 = 15.0/28.02 = 0.535. Moles H2 = 15.0/2.02 = 7.43.
N2 is limiting. 0.535 mol N2 * (2 mol NH3 / 1 mol N2) * (17.03 g/mol) = 18.2 g NH3.
