January 2, 2026: Trigonometry Foundations & Periodicity

Section 1: BIG IDEA

This session explores the structural relationships within the unit circle and the oscillatory nature of trigonometric functions. We examine how periodic activity, specifically within the sine and tangent layers, creates predictable patterns like frequency and amplitude. Furthermore, the concept of homeostatic periodicity is introduced, illustrating how mathematical systems (and biological ones) use feedback loops to return to a set point of 360° or 2π.

Section 3: STEPS/PROCESS

  1. Identify the Layer: Determine if the oscillation is occurring in the sine layer (periodic activity) or the tangent layer (asymptotic activity).
  2. Apply Transformations: Execute horizontal translations (phase shifts) first, followed by vertical translations.
  3. Determine Periodicity: Calculate the frequency molecules derived from the energy of the period to establish harmonic motion.
  4. Evaluate the Range: Use the central value system for inverse functions to determine the valid domain and range cells.

Section 4: RULES/IMPORTANT NOTES

  • SOHCAHTOA: A primary mnemonic for right triangle ratios (Sine=Opp/Hyp, Cosine=Adj/Hyp, Tangent=Opp/Adj).
  • The "Bear Rule": Sine and Cosine exist "under the house" (within the primary structure), while Secant and Cosecant are reciprocals.
  • Set Points: The standard set point for a full rotation is 360°, with a normal functional range typically between 359.5° and 361°.
  • Negative Phase: Acts as a corrective force to bring a system back to its normal range when amplitude becomes "out of whack."
