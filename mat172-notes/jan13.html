Date: January 13, 2026
Instructor: Dr. Mendoor (Jean)

BIG IDEA

MAT-172 (Pre-Calculus Trigonometry) builds upon MAT-171 foundations to explore the relationships between angles and triangles, circular functions, and analytic trigonometry. Success in this STEM-track course requires rigorous organization, handwritten verification of digital work, and mastery of the unit circle.

KEY TERMS

Term Definition
\(\theta, \alpha, \beta\) Greek letters commonly used as variables to represent angle measures.
Radian A unit of angle measure based on the radius of a circle; alternative to degrees.
Unit Circle A circle with radius \(r = 1\) used to define trigonometric functions.
Oblique Triangle A triangle that does not contain a right (\(90^\circ\)) angle.
Hypotenuse The side opposite the right angle in a right triangle; represented as \(c\).

TRIGONOMETRIC FOUNDATIONS

The Pythagorean Theorem: Applicable only to right triangles: \[a^2 + b^2 = c^2\]

In trigonometry, we label sides relative to a specific acute angle \(\theta\):

  1. Hypotenuse: Always the longest side, opposite the \(90^\circ\) angle.
  2. Opposite: The side directly across from angle \(\theta\).
  3. Adjacent: The side "beside" or touching angle \(\theta\) (that is not the hypotenuse).

EXAMPLE: SOH-CAH-TOA DEFINITIONS

1. Primary Functions:
  • \(\sin(\theta) = \frac{\text{Opposite}}{\text{Hypotenuse}}\)
  • \(\cos(\theta) = \frac{\text{Adjacent}}{\text{Hypotenuse}}\)
  • \(\tan(\theta) = \frac{\text{Opposite}}{\text{Adjacent}}\)
2. Reciprocal Functions:
  • \(\csc(\theta) = \frac{1}{\sin(\theta)}\)
  • \(\sec(\theta) = \frac{1}{\cos(\theta)}\)
  • \(\cot(\theta) = \frac{1}{\tan(\theta)}\)
3. Key Conversion:

The relationship between degrees and radians is anchored by the straight angle:

\[180^\circ = \pi \text{ radians}\]

PRACTICE PROBLEMS

  1. If a right triangle has legs \(a = 3\) and \(b = 4\), find the hypotenuse \(c\). (Answer: 5)
  2. Convert \(90^\circ\) to radians using the ratio \(\frac{\pi}{180^\circ}\). (Answer: \(\frac{\pi}{2}\))
  3. Identify the reciprocal of \(\cos(\theta)\). (Answer: \(\sec(\theta)\))
