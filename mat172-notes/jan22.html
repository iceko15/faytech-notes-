Co-functions, Reference Angles & Quadrantal Values

Resource Note: Check the "Learning Resources" folder in Canvas. I've uploaded "Chapter 4 Examples PDF" containing MyMathLab samples and diagrams. The "Green Packet" (page 14-19) is critical for study guides and unit circle charts.

BIG IDEA

While reciprocals flip the ratio of a function, Co-functions relate two different functions based on complementary angles (totaling $90^\circ$ or $\pi/2$). Evaluating trig functions of any angle requires mastering reference angles and quadrant-specific signs (ASTC).

KEY TERMS

  • Reciprocal: A pair of functions where the entire SOH-CAH-TOA ratio is flipped (e.g., $\sin$ and $\csc$). It is not the angle that reciprocates.
  • Co-function: Functions that share values when their angles are complementary.
  • Complementary Angles: Two positive acute angles that sum to $90^\circ$ or $\pi/2$.
  • Quadrantal Angle: Angles that terminate on the axes ($0^\circ, 90^\circ, 180^\circ, 270^\circ$).
  • Reference Angle ($\theta'$): The positive acute angle formed by the terminal side of $\theta$ and the x-axis.

RULES & IDENTITIES

1. Co-function Pairings

FunctionCo-function PairIdentity (Radians)
Sine ($\sin$)Cosine ($\cos$)$\sin(\theta) = \cos(\frac{\pi}{2} - \theta)$
Secant ($\sec$)Cosecant ($\csc$)$\sec(\theta) = \csc(\frac{\pi}{2} - \theta)$
Tangent ($\tan$)Cotangent ($\cot$)$\tan(\theta) = \cot(\frac{\pi}{2} - \theta)$

2. Quadrant Signs (ASTC)

All Students Take Calculus:

  • Quadrant I (All): All functions are positive.
  • Quadrant II (Students): Only $\sin$ (and $\csc$) are positive.
  • Quadrant III (Take): Only $\tan$ (and $\cot$) are positive.
  • Quadrant IV (Calculus): Only $\cos$ (and $\sec$) are positive.

3. Reference Angle Calculations

  • Q1: $\theta' = \theta$
  • Q2: $\theta' = 180^\circ - \theta$ or $\pi - \theta$
  • Q3: $\theta' = \theta - 180^\circ$ or $\theta - \pi$
  • Q4: $\theta' = 360^\circ - \theta$ or $2\pi - \theta$

CO-FUNCTION EXAMPLES

Example 5a: Convert $\sin(46^\circ)$ to its co-function.
Calculation: $\cos(90^\circ - 46^\circ) = \cos(44^\circ)$.
Example 5b: Convert $\cot(\frac{\pi}{12})$ to its co-function.
Setup: $\tan(\frac{\pi}{2} - \frac{\pi}{12})$.
LCD Step: Multiply $\frac{\pi}{2}$ by $\frac{6}{6} \rightarrow \frac{6\pi}{12}$.
Arithmetic: $\frac{6\pi}{12} - \frac{\pi}{12} = \frac{5\pi}{12}$.
Result: $\tan(\frac{5\pi}{12})$.

SOLVING RIGHT TRIANGLES (WORD PROBLEM)

Scenario: A right triangle with an angle of $24^\circ$ and an adjacent side of 750. Find the opposite side ($a$).
1. Identify sides: We have Adjacent (750) and need Opposite ($a$). Use Tangent.
2. Equation: $\tan(24^\circ) = \frac{a}{750}$.
3. Solve: $a = 750 \cdot \tan(24^\circ)$.
4. Calculator Check: Ensure mode is in DEGREES.
5. Result: $a \approx 333.9$.
Sanity Check: Visually, $24^\circ$ is a shallow angle. The opposite side should be less than the adjacent side (roughly half). If you get a negative number or a massive number like 3339, you either have a mode error (Radians vs. Degrees) or a decimal error.

TRIG FUNCTIONS OF ANY ANGLE

Point Value: Let $P = (1, -3)$. Find the six trig functions.
1. $x = 1$ (Adjacent), $y = -3$ (Opposite).
2. Find $r$ (Hypotenuse): $r = \sqrt{1^2 + (-3)^2} = \sqrt{10} \approx 3.2$.
3. Sine: $\frac{-3}{\sqrt{10}} = \frac{-3\sqrt{10}}{10}$.
4. Cosine: $\frac{1}{\sqrt{10}} = \frac{\sqrt{10}}{10}$.
5. Tangent: $\frac{-3}{1} = -3$.
6. Reciprocals: $\csc = \frac{\sqrt{10}}{-3}$, $\sec = \sqrt{10}$, $\cot = -\frac{1}{3}$.

QUADRANTAL ANGLES & UNDEFINED VALUES

Example: Find $\csc(180^\circ)$.
1. On the unit circle, $180^\circ$ is the point $(-1, 0)$.
2. $\csc = \frac{1}{\sin} = \frac{\text{Hypotenuse}}{\text{Opposite}}$.
3. Here, Opposite ($y$) is $0$.
4. Result: $\frac{1}{0} = \mathbf{Undefined}$.
In Calculus, you will deal with division by zero, but for now, "Undefined" (UNDEF) is the only acceptable answer. "No answer" is too vague.

REFERENCE ANGLE PRACTICE

  • $\theta = 210^\circ$: Q3. Distance to x-axis ($180^\circ$). $210 - 180 = \mathbf{30^\circ}$.
  • $\theta = -240^\circ$: Terminating in Q2. $|-240| = 240$. $240 - 180 = \mathbf{60^\circ}$.
  • $\theta = 665^\circ$: Remove one loop ($360^\circ$). $665 - 360 = 305^\circ$ (Q4). $360 - 305 = \mathbf{55^\circ}$.
  • $\theta = -\frac{11\pi}{3}$: Add two revolutions ($4\pi$ or $\frac{12\pi}{3}$). Result is $\frac{\pi}{3}$, which is in Q1 and serves as its own reference angle.

SUMMARY

Reference angles allow us to pull values from the unit circle (the "bow tie" visual) for any rotation. Always identify the quadrant first to determine if your final value should be positive or negative (ASTC). Co-functions are a separate algebraic manipulation tool used mostly for identities and expressions.
