Verifying Trigonometric Identities

BIG IDEA

Verifying an identity is an algebraic proof where you manipulate one side of an equation until it mathematically transforms into the other side. Unlike solving equations, we are not moving terms across the equals sign; we are rewriting equivalent expressions to show why a statement is true.

Professor's Warning: The work IS the answer. On a test, simply writing "True" will get zero credit. You must show the logical flow of every substitution and algebraic step.

KEY TERMS

  • LHS / RHS: Left-hand side and Right-hand side of the identity.
  • Verification: The process of using established identities to prove a new statement.
  • Conjugate: Multiplying a numerator or denominator by its opposite sign (e.g., $1 - \sin x$ has the conjugate $1 + \sin x$) to force a Pythagorean identity.
  • LCD: Lowest Common Denominator, required for adding or subtracting trigonometric fractions.

CORE RULES & IDENTITIES

Fundamental Toolbox (Chapter 4 Review)

Type Identities
Reciprocal $\csc x = \frac{1}{\sin x}$, $\sec x = \frac{1}{\cos x}$, $\cot x = \frac{1}{\tan x}$
Quotient $\tan x = \frac{\sin x}{\cos x}$, $\cot x = \frac{\cos x}{\sin x}$
Pythagorean $\sin^2 x + \cos^2 x = 1$
$\tan^2 x + 1 = \sec^2 x$
$1 + \cot^2 x = \csc^2 x$
Even/Odd $\sin(-x) = -\sin x$
$\cos(-x) = \cos x$
$\tan(-x) = -\tan x$
Algebraic Reminder: Pythagorean identities are often rearranged. For example, if you see $1 - \sin^2 x$, you can immediately substitute $\cos^2 x$. If you see $1 - \cos^2 x$, you can substitute $\sin^2 x$.

STEPS/PROCESS FOR VERIFICATION

  1. Start with the more complicated side: It is generally easier to "tear down" a complex expression into a simpler one.
  2. Convert to Sines and Cosines: If you are stuck, rewrite all functions (secant, cosecant, tangent, cotangent) in terms of $\sin x$ and $\cos x$.
  3. Perform Algebraic Operations:
    • Factor out a GCF (Greatest Common Factor).
    • Add/Subtract fractions by finding an LCD.
    • Multiply by a version of "1" (the conjugate).
    • Foil/Distribute binomials.
  4. Keep the Goal in Mind: Constantly look at the "other" side to see if your manipulations are getting you closer to that target.

EXAMPLE 1: Simple Substitution

Verify: $\csc x \tan x = \sec x$

1. Start with LHS: $\csc x \tan x$

2. Rewrite in terms of sine/cosine: $$\left(\frac{1}{\sin x}\right) \left(\frac{\sin x}{\cos x}\right)$$

3. Simplify: The $\sin x$ in the numerator and denominator cancel out. $$\frac{1}{\cos x}$$

4. Apply Reciprocal Identity: $$\sec x$$

Result: LHS = RHS. Proof complete.

EXAMPLE 2: Using the LCD and Pythagorean Substitution

Verify: $\csc \theta - \sin \theta = \cot \theta \cos \theta$

1. Start with LHS: $\frac{1}{\sin \theta} - \sin \theta$

2. Find LCD: Put $\sin \theta$ over 1 and multiply by $\frac{\sin \theta}{\sin \theta}$. $$\frac{1}{\sin \theta} - \frac{\sin^2 \theta}{\sin \theta} = \frac{1 - \sin^2 \theta}{\sin \theta}$$

3. Pythagorean Substitution: $1 - \sin^2 \theta = \cos^2 \theta$. $$\frac{\cos^2 \theta}{\sin \theta}$$

4. Split for Logic: $\cos^2 \theta = \cos \theta \cdot \cos \theta$. $$\left(\frac{\cos \theta}{\sin \theta}\right) \cdot \cos \theta$$

5. Result: $\cot \theta \cos \theta = \text{RHS}$.

EXAMPLE 3: GCF and Factoring

Verify: $\sec x - \sec x \sin^2 x = \cos x$

1. Factor GCF: Pull out $\sec x$. $$\sec x (1 - \sin^2 x)$$

2. Substitution: $1 - \sin^2 x = \cos^2 x$. $$\sec x \cdot \cos^2 x$$

3. Reciprocal Identity: $\sec x = \frac{1}{\cos x}$. $$\left(\frac{1}{\cos x}\right) \cdot \cos^2 x = \frac{\cos \cdot \cos}{\cos}$$

4. Simplify: $\cos x = \text{RHS}$.

COMPLEX EXAMPLE: Labor Intensive Verification

Verify: $\frac{\cos x}{1 - \sin x} + \frac{1 - \sin x}{\cos x} = 2 \sec x$

1. Find LCD: $(1 - \sin x)(\cos x)$. $$\frac{\cos^2 x + (1 - \sin x)^2}{(1 - \sin x)(\cos x)}$$

2. FOIL Numerator: $(1 - \sin x)(1 - \sin x) = 1 - 2\sin x + \sin^2 x$. $$\frac{\cos^2 x + 1 - 2\sin x + \sin^2 x}{(1 - \sin x)(\cos x)}$$

3. Identify Pythagorean Pair: $\cos^2 x + \sin^2 x = 1$. $$\frac{(1) + 1 - 2\sin x}{(1 - \sin x)(\cos x)} = \frac{2 - 2\sin x}{(1 - \sin x)(\cos x)}$$

4. Factor Numerator: $$\frac{2(1 - \sin x)}{(1 - \sin x)(\cos x)}$$

5. Cancel and Finalize: The $(1 - \sin x)$ terms cancel. $$\frac{2}{\cos x} = 2 \sec x$$

Study Tip: Don't try to memorize specific problems. Focus on the strategies: "When did I factor? When did I find an LCD? When did I use a Pythagorean identity?"

MYMATHLAB HURDLES

  • Strict Variables: If the problem uses $\theta$, do not use $x$. If it uses $t$, do not use $T$.
  • Auto-Format: When you type cos or tan, the software will bold the text to show it recognizes the function.
  • Step-by-Step Flow: In "Fill in the Box" problems, you must follow the software's specific logic path, even if you would have solved it differently on paper.
  • Instructions: Pay attention to "Do not simplify" vs. "Simplify as much as possible."

SUMMARY

Verification is an exercise in seeing algebra through the "fog" of trigonometry. Success requires fluency in the three core Pythagorean identities and basic algebraic skills like factoring and fraction addition. Always keep one eye on the target side to guide your next move.
