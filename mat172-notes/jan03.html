Trigonometric Layers & System Periodicity

[cite_start]

This lecture synthesizes advanced trigonometric identities with the conceptual framework of harmonic motion and biological "periodicity." [cite: 7, 42]

BIG IDEA

Mathematical periodicity functions as a "set point" for systems. [cite_start]Just as a circle returns to 360°, systems use negative feedback to return to a normal range when oscillations become too high or low. [cite: 97, 102, 119]

RULES: SYSTEM EQUILIBRIUM

    [cite_start]
  • Positive Phase: Makes a reaction or oscillation greater; less common in stable systems. [cite: 115, 121]
    • [cite_start]
  • Negative Phase: Works to bring the system back to periodicity by making the reaction lesser. [cite: 118, 119]
    • [cite_start]
  • The Set Point: For a full circle, the set point is 360°; variations within a small range (e.g., 359.5° to 361°) are normal. [cite: 97, 100, 102]
    • [cite_start]
  • Out of Range: Once a value exceeds 370°, the system is "out of whack" and requires other mathematical areas to kick in. [cite: 101, 109, 111]

Every mathematical graph and biological oscillation is working to maintain a set point. [cite_start]Whether managing amplitude in the "saline" or tracking the horizontal oxidation of a wave, the goal is to return to the periodic normal range. [cite: 5, 44, 111, 120]
