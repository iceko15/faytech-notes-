BIG IDEA

Complex numbers, typically expressed in rectangular form ($a + bi$), can be represented in polar form ($r(\cos \theta + i \sin \theta)$). This representation significantly simplifies operations like multiplication, division, and especially raising numbers to high powers (De Moivre’s Theorem).

KEY TERMS

  • Modulus ($r$): The absolute value of the complex number, representing the distance from the origin. Calculated as $r = \sqrt{a^2 + b^2}$.
  • Argument ($\theta$): The angle in standard position. For polar form, $\theta$ must be between $0 \le \theta < 360^\circ$ (or $2\pi$).
  • Rectangular Form: $z = a + bi$ (where $a$ is horizontal/real and $b$ is vertical/imaginary).
  • Polar Form: $z = r(\cos \theta + i \sin \theta)$, often abbreviated as $r \text{ cis } \theta$.
  • Co-terminal Angles: If a calculation results in an angle outside the $0-360^\circ$ range, you must add or subtract $360^\circ$ to find the standard revolution equivalent.

RULES & IDENTITIES

Operations in Polar Form

Given $z_1 = r_1(\cos \theta_1 + i \sin \theta_1)$ and $z_2 = r_2(\cos \theta_2 + i \sin \theta_2)$:

  • Product: $z_1 z_2 = r_1 r_2 [\cos(\theta_1 + \theta_2) + i \sin(\theta_1 + \theta_2)]$
  • Quotient: $\frac{z_1}{z_2} = \frac{r_1}{r_2} [\cos(\theta_1 - \theta_2) + i \sin(\theta_1 - \theta_2)]$
  • De Moivre’s Theorem (Powers): $z^n = r^n [\cos(n\theta) + i \sin(n\theta)]$
  • Roots of Complex Numbers: $w_k = \sqrt[n]{r} \left[ \cos\left(\frac{\theta + 360^\circ k}{n}\right) + i \sin\left(\frac{\theta + 360^\circ k}{n}\right) \right]$ for $k = 0, 1, 2, \dots, n-1$.

EXAMPLE 1: Product of Complex Numbers

Problem: Find the product of $z_1 = 4(\cos \frac{\pi}{5} + i \sin \frac{\pi}{5})$ and $z_2 = 3(\cos \frac{\pi}{10} + i \sin \frac{\pi}{10})$. Leave in polar form.

Step 1: Multiply the $r$ values.
$4 \times 3 = 12$

Step 2: Add the angles ($\theta$).
$\frac{\pi}{5} + \frac{\pi}{10} = \frac{2\pi}{10} + \frac{\pi}{10} = \frac{3\pi}{10}$

Final Polar Answer:
$12(\cos \frac{3\pi}{10} + i \sin \frac{3\pi}{10})$

EXAMPLE 2: De Moivre’s Theorem (Powers)

Problem: Calculate $[4(\cos 15^\circ + i \sin 15^\circ)]^3$. Convert to rectangular form.

Step 1: Apply the power to $r$.
$r^3 = 4^3 = 64$

Step 2: Multiply the angle by $n$.
$3 \times 15^\circ = 45^\circ$

Step 3: Convert to Rectangular Form using unit circle values.
$64(\cos 45^\circ + i \sin 45^\circ) = 64(\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2} + i \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2})$
$= 32\sqrt{2} + 32\sqrt{2}i$

EXAMPLE 3: Rectangular to Polar to Power

Problem: Find $(1 + i)^5$ using De Moivre’s Theorem.

Step 1: Convert $1 + i$ to Polar.
$r = \sqrt{1^2 + 1^2} = \sqrt{2}$
$\theta = \tan^{-1}(\frac{1}{1}) = 45^\circ$ (Quadrant I)

Step 2: Apply the Theorem for $n=5$.
$(\sqrt{2})^5 [\cos(5 \times 45^\circ) + i \sin(5 \times 45^\circ)]$
Radical Simplification Guide:
$(\sqrt{2})^5 = \sqrt{2} \cdot \sqrt{2} \cdot \sqrt{2} \cdot \sqrt{2} \cdot \sqrt{2}$
$= (\sqrt{2} \cdot \sqrt{2}) \cdot (\sqrt{2} \cdot \sqrt{2}) \cdot \sqrt{2}$
$= 2 \cdot 2 \cdot \sqrt{2} = \mathbf{4\sqrt{2}}$
Note: Exact form is required for exams. No decimals!
$r^5 = 4\sqrt{2}$
$\theta = 225^\circ$

Step 3: Solve for Exact Rectangular Form.
$225^\circ$ is in Quadrant III ($\cos, \sin$ are negative).
$4\sqrt{2} [-\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2} - i \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}] = -4 - 4i$
Verification Strategy: The Decimal Match: Exact radicals are required for credit, but use your TI-Nspire to compare the decimal value of your final answer to the trig expression. If the decimals don't match, your radical simplification is wrong!

COMPLEX ROOTS (PREVIEW)

To find the $n$-th roots of a complex number, you must find $n$ distinct answers. For example, the 4th roots of $-16i$ will yield 4 separate complex numbers.

  • Degree Rule: The number of roots equals the degree of the root (e.g., cube root = 3 answers).
  • Iteration: Use $k = 0, 1, 2...$ in the formula until you reach $n-1$.

Section: Visualizing N-th Roots

The 'Circle of Roots' Concept: N-th roots of a complex number are spaced perfectly evenly around a circle in the complex plane, separated by an angle of $360^\circ/n$.

  • Example: 3rd roots form a triangle ($120^\circ$ apart).
  • Example: 4th roots form a square ($90^\circ$ apart).

SUMMARY

  • Polar Form: $z = r(\cos \theta + i \sin \theta)$.
  • Multiplication: Multiply $r$'s, Add $\theta$'s.
  • Division: Divide $r$'s, Subtract $\theta$'s.
  • Powers: $r$ to the power, $n$ times the $\theta$.
  • Check: Use the calculator to compare decimal approximations of your exact radical answers.
Exam Strategy: When finding roots, check your spacing! If your 4th roots aren't exactly $90^\circ$ apart, your arithmetic has gone "off the rails".
