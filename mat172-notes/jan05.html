Trigonometry Foundations & Periodicity

BIG IDEA

Understanding the interplay between the unit circle, trigonometric identities, and the concept of periodicity. This session emphasizes how circular motion translates into oscillating waves and the importance of maintaining a "normal range" or set point within periodic functions.

KEY TERMS

Term Definition
Unit Circle A circle with a radius of \( r = 1 \), used to define trigonometric functions.
Asymptotes Lines that a graph approaches but never touches, specifically noted in the tangent layer.
Coterminal Angles Angles that share the same terminal side but differ by an integer number of full rotations.
Phase Shift The horizontal translation of a periodic function, represented by the variable \( C \).
Periodicity The quality of a function repeating its values in regular intervals or cycles.

STEPS/PROCESS

  1. Identify the Terminal Side: Rotate the angle \( \theta \) from the standard position and slide to the reference triangle model.
  2. Apply SOHCAHTOA: Use the relationship of sides (\( \sin = \frac{opp}{hyp} \), \( \cos = \frac{adj}{hyp} \), \( \tan = \frac{opp}{adj} \)) to find ratios.
  3. Convert to Radians: Use the direct conversion factor \( \pi = 180^\circ \).
  4. Analyze Wave Layers: Determine the amplitude and crest/trough values for sine and cosine oscillations.
  5. Solve Oblique Triangles: Select the appropriate law (Law of Sines or Law of Cosines) based on given parts (\( SAS, ASA, SSS \)).

RULES

  • The Pythagorean Identity: Always holds that \( \sin^2(\theta) + \cos^2(\theta) = 1 \).
  • Horizontal First: In transformations, address the phase shift (horizontal) before vertical translations.
  • Periodicity Normal Range: A full circle set point is \( 360^\circ \). Variations within \( 359.5^\circ \) to \( 361^\circ \) are considered a "normal range."
  • Phase Reactions:
    • Positive Phase: Increases the magnitude of the reaction.
    • Negative Phase: Lessens the reaction to bring the system back to periodicity.

EXAMPLE/DEMONSTRATION

Example 1: Unit Circle Coordinates
If a point lies on the unit circle at \( 60^\circ \), the coordinates are \( (\frac{1}{2}, \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}) \). Here, \( \cos(60^\circ) = \frac{1}{2} \) and \( \sin(60^\circ) = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} \).
Example 2: Frequency Breakdown
The period \( T \) of harmonic motion can be broken down into frequency molecules. The relationship is defined as \( f = \frac{1}{T} \).
Example 3: pH Scale Comparison
Trig periodicity is compared to pH: Acidic solutions (high \( H^+ \)) have a low pH value (e.g., \( 0-2 \)), while Basic solutions (high \( OH^- \)) have high pH values.
