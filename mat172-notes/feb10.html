KEY TERMS

  • $\sin^{-1}$ (Inverse Sine): Not a power! This is function notation representing the angle whose sine is $x$.
  • Phase Shift: The horizontal shift of a trigonometric graph, calculated by setting the argument of the function equal to zero.
  • Periodic Nature: Because trig functions repeat, multiple equations can represent the same graph (e.g., shifting $\pi/2$ left vs. $\pi/2$ right for tangent).
  • Domain Restriction: The specific interval where a trig function is "one-to-one" so that its inverse can exist.

INVERSE RANGE RESTRICTIONS

The calculator is "trained" to only provide answers within these specific ranges. If your manual calculation falls outside these, it is likely a quadrant error.

Function Interval (Range) Quadrants Involved
$\sin^{-1}(x)$ $[-\pi/2, \pi/2]$ I and IV (Negative IV)
$\cos^{-1}(x)$ $[0, \pi]$ I and II
$\tan^{-1}(x)$ $(-\pi/2, \pi/2)$ I and IV (Endpoints excluded)

STEPS/PROCESS: Matching Equations to Graphs

  1. Identify General Shape: Positive tangent goes "uphill"; negative tangent (or cotangent) goes "downhill."
  2. Find the Period: For tangent, the period is $\pi/b$. Look for the distance between asymptotes or the distance for one full cycle.
  3. Check for Vertical Shift ($d$): Does the center of the swoop pass through the x-axis? If not, $d \neq 0$.
  4. Calculate Phase Shift: Identify where the "origin point" of the base graph has moved. Solve the inner parenthesis for zero to verify.

RULES/IDENTITIES

  • Inverse vs. Reciprocal: $x^{-1} = \frac{1}{x}$ (Reciprocal), but $\sin^{-1}(x) \neq \frac{1}{\sin(x)}$. $\frac{1}{\sin(x)}$ is $\csc(x)$.
  • Pythagorean Theorem for Missing Sides: $a^2 + b^2 = c^2$. Remember that the hypotenuse ($c$) is always positive in trig sketches.
  • Rationalizing Denominators: Simplify radicals before rationalizing to avoid oversized numbers. Example: $\sqrt{48} = 4\sqrt{3}$.

EXAMPLE: Composite Inverse Functions

Problem: Find the exact value of $\sin^{-1}(\sin(\frac{5\pi}{6}))$.

Step 1: Work from the inside out. $\sin(\frac{5\pi}{6})$ is in Quadrant II. The reference angle is $\pi/6$. In QII, sine is positive. $\sin(\frac{5\pi}{6}) = \frac{1}{2}$.

Step 2: Apply the inverse. Now we need $\sin^{-1}(\frac{1}{2})$.
The range for $\sin^{-1}$ is restricted to Quadrants I and IV ($[-\pi/2, \pi/2]$). Sine is positive $1/2$ in Quadrant I at $\pi/6$.

Final Answer: $\pi/6$.
Note: The functions do NOT simply "cancel out" because the original angle ($5\pi}{6}$) was outside the restricted range of the inverse function.

EXAMPLE: Non-Unit Circle Inverse

Problem: Evaluate $\sec(\sin^{-1}(-\frac{3}{8}))$.

Step 1: Sketch the Triangle. Since the input to $\sin^{-1}$ is negative, we draw the triangle in Quadrant IV (the restricted range for sine). Opposite = $-3$, Hypotenuse = $8$.

Step 2: Find the Adjacent Side. $adj^2 + (-3)^2 = 8^2 \Rightarrow adj^2 + 9 = 64 \Rightarrow adj = \sqrt{55}$.

Step 3: Find Secant. Secant is $\frac{hypotenuse}{adjacent}$. $\sec = \frac{8}{\sqrt{55}}$.

Step 4: Rationalize. $\frac{8\sqrt{55}}{55}$.

EXAMPLE: Navigational Bearing Word Problem

Problem: Ranger 1 sites a fire directly South. Ranger 2 is 6 miles East of Ranger 1 and sites the same fire at a bearing of South 20° West. How far is Ranger 1 from the fire?

Setup: - Ranger 1 (R1) and Fire (F) form a vertical line. - R1 and Ranger 2 (R2) form a horizontal line of 6 miles. - This creates a right triangle at R1. - The bearing at R2 is S 20° W. The interior angle of the triangle at R2 is $90^\circ - 20^\circ = 70^\circ$.

Calculation: Using the $70^\circ$ angle at R2: Opposite = distance from R1 to fire ($x$). Adjacent = 6 miles. $\tan(70^\circ) = \frac{x}{6}$ $x = 6 \cdot \tan(70^\circ) \approx 16.5$ miles.

SUMMARY

  • Check your calculator MODE (Radians vs. Degrees) every time you touch a trig button.
  • For inverse functions, if the input is not between -1 and 1 (for sine/cosine), the result is "Undefined" or "Domain Error."
  • Always identify the quadrant first when drawing triangles for word problems or inverse composites.
  • Solving a triangle means finding all six parts (3 sides, 3 angles).
