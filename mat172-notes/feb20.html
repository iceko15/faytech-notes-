BIG IDEA

Success in Chapter 3 and beyond relies heavily on mastery of prerequisite algebraic skills from MAT-171, specifically factoring and simplifying rational expressions. Understanding "under the hood" mechanics is essential as the course progresses into more complex rational expression setups.

KEY TERMS

  • Skills Check: An online review of foundational material designed to ensure readiness for the next steps in the curriculum.
  • Factoring: The process of breaking down a polynomial into a product of simpler polynomials.
  • Rational Expressions: Fractions where the numerator and denominator are polynomials.

STEPS/PROCESS

While specific new problems weren't solved on the board in this transcript snippet, the professor outlined the required workflow for the upcoming units:

  1. Complete the Skills Check to identify gaps in MAT-171 knowledge.
  2. Focus heavily on Factoring; this skill is utilized extensively in Chapter P, Chapter 2, and Chapter 3.
  3. Apply factoring to Rational Expression setups, which are prevalent throughout the current coursework.

RULES/IDENTITIES

The professor emphasized that the following MAT-171 concepts are non-negotiable for Chapter 3:

  • Basic Polynomial Factoring (Trinomials, Difference of Squares).
  • Simplifying Rational Expressions.
