Graphing Trig Functions & Transformations

Professor's Warning: MyMathLab often overdoes the calculations in their "Show Me How" sections. They will have you writing pages of overkill math. We are going to use a much more efficient approach: identifying landmarks (maxes, mins, intercepts) and breaking periods into quarters.

BIG IDEA

The core of graphing trigonometric functions is recognizing that they are periodic—meaning they repeat a specific "chunk" or cycle infinitely. If you can graph one period correctly using five landmark points, you can simply "copy and paste" the graph to the left and right.

KEY TERMS

  • Period: The smallest horizontal interval over which the function completes one full cycle. For Sine and Cosine, the base period is $2\pi$. For Tangent and Cotangent, it is $\pi$.
  • Amplitude: Half the distance between the maximum and minimum values. It measures how tall the "lumps or bumps" are from the midline.
  • Midline: The horizontal line ($y = D$) that the graph oscillates around.
  • Phase Shift: The horizontal shift (left or right) caused by a value inside the angle structure.
  • Landmark Points: The five key values in one period: the beginning, the end, the midpoint, and the two quarter-marks.

BASE MODELS (ONE PERIOD)

Function Base Period Domain Range Standard Landmarks
$y = \sin(x)$ $2\pi$ $(-\infty, \infty)$ $[-1, 1]$ Starts at $(0,0)$, Max at $\pi/2$, Crosses at $\pi$, Min at $3\pi/2$, Ends at $2\pi$.
$y = \cos(x)$ $2\pi$ $(-\infty, \infty)$ $[-1, 1]$ Starts at $(0,1)$, Crosses at $\pi/2$, Min at $\pi$, Crosses at $3\pi/2$, Ends at $(2\pi, 1)$.
$y = \tan(x)$ $\pi$ $x \neq \frac{\pi}{2} + n\pi$ $(-\infty, \infty)$ Asymptotes at $-\pi/2$ and $\pi/2$; Crosses $(0,0)$.

THE TRANSFORMATION TEMPLATE (ABCD)

We approach every equation using the template: $$y = A \cdot \text{trig}(B(x - C)) + D$$

  • A (Amplitude/Reflection):
    • If negative: Reflection about the x-axis.
    • Value: Vertical stretch ($|A| > 1$) or shrink ($0 < |A| < 1$).
  • B (Period Modifier):
    • Horizontal stretch or shrink.
    • Formula for Period: $P = \frac{\text{Base Period}}{B}$.
  • C (Phase Shift):
    • Horizontal shift. Pro-tip: To find the exact shift with signs included, take the entire chunk in parentheses and solve for $x = 0$.
    • Minus $C$ moves Right; Plus $C$ moves Left (it feels backwards).
  • D (Vertical Shift):
    • Moves the graph up or down. This shift is always the very last action you take.

ORDER OF OPERATIONS FOR GRAPHING

  1. Identify the Phase Shift (Left/Right) first.
  2. Apply Stretching/Shrinking (Horizontal and Vertical).
  3. Apply Reflections (x or y axis).
  4. Apply Vertical Shifting (Up/Down) last.
Scaling Your Paper: Don't just draw random squiggles. For the horizontal axis, I recommend using multiples of four boxes for one period. Since we break everything into quarters, this makes plotting the landmark points simple.

EXAMPLE: $y = 2\sin(\frac{1}{2}x)$

Step 1: Identify Transformations

  • $A = 2$: The Amplitude is 2. The graph will reach 2 and -2.
  • $B = 1/2$: This is a horizontal stretch.

Step 2: Calculate Period

$$P = \frac{2\pi}{1/2} = 2\pi \cdot \frac{2}{1} = 4\pi$$

Step 3: Determine Landmark Points (Quarter Marks)

Divide the $4\pi$ period into 4 equal chunks: $4\pi / 4 = \pi$.

  • $0$: Start (Intercept) $\rightarrow (0,0)$
  • $\pi$: First Quarter (Max) $\rightarrow (\pi, 2)$
  • $2\pi$: Middle (Intercept) $\rightarrow (2\pi, 0)$
  • $3\pi$: Third Quarter (Min) $\rightarrow (3\pi, -2)$
  • $4\pi$: End (Intercept) $\rightarrow (4\pi, 0)$

SUMMARY FOR MYMATHLAB

  • The software is multiple choice for most graphs, but it requires you to click transformations in the correct chronological order to get credit.
  • Don't fixate on 12,000 calculations; focus on the midline, intercepts, and max/min points.
  • Tangent and Cotangent have asymptotes wherever the denominator (of the $\frac{\sin}{\cos}$ or $\frac{\cos}{\sin}$ ratio) equals zero.
